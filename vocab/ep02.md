# Episode 02

## worth
- **adj.** valuable enough to justify the cost, effort, or risk — used as "be worth + noun/V-ing"
  - 例：It's worth the effort.（值得付出。）
  - 例：This book is worth reading.（這本書值得一讀。）
  - 例：Is it really worth the trouble?（真的值得那麼麻煩嗎？）
  - 常見搭配：worth the risk（值得冒險）、worth the wait（值得等待）、worth it（值得的）、worth + V-ing（值得做某事）
- **dialogue:** A little danger is worth the risk, don't you think?
  - Jayce 對 Caitlyn 裝帥，說跑去底城冒點險是值得的——結果幾秒後公寓就被炸了，格外諷刺

## break-in
- **n.** an illegal entry into a building, usually to steal something; the noun form of "break in"
  - 例：There was a break-in at the office last night.（辦公室昨晚被闖入了。）
  - 例：Police are investigating a series of break-ins.（警方正在調查一系列闖入事件。）
  - 補充：動詞是 break in / break into（闖入），名詞加連字號變 break-in
- **dialogue:** We know it was a break-in but that doesn't explain this.
  - Grayson 承認有人闖入，但話鋒一轉——闖入歸闖入，你家裡這些違禁品又怎麼解釋？Jayce 從受害者瞬間變嫌疑人

## restricted
- **adj.** limited or controlled by rules or laws; not freely available to the public
  - 例：This is a restricted area.（這是管制區域。）
  - 例：Access to the files is restricted.（這些檔案的存取受到限制。）
  - 常見搭配：restricted area（管制區）、restricted items（管制物品）、restricted access（限制進入）、restricted information（機密資訊）
- **dialogue:** There's a lot of restricted items here.
  - Grayson 指出 Jayce 家裡有大量管制物品——在 Piltover 持有這些東西需要許可，Jayce 顯然沒有

## permit
- **n.** an official document that gives you permission to do something or have something
  - 例：You need a permit to park here.（你需要停車許可證才能停這裡。）
  - 例：a building permit（建築許可）、a work permit（工作許可）
  - 常見搭配：apply for a permit（申請許可）、issue a permit（發放許可）、without a permit（未經許可）
- **dialogue:** I don't see any permits.
  - Grayson 說沒看到任何許可證——暗示 Jayce 是非法持有這些管制物品

## independent study
- **n.** a self-directed research project not supervised by a professor or part of a formal course; in academic settings, a student chooses their own topic and works on it alone
  - 例：She did an independent study on climate change.（她做了一個關於氣候變遷的自主研究。）
  - 例：The university offers independent study for senior students.（這所大學為高年級學生提供自主研究課程。）
- **dialogue:** It was an independent study.
  - Viktor 問「誰授權你做研究的」，Jayce 回答是自主研究——等於承認沒有任何人批准，自己挖坑跳

## assistant
- **n.** a person who helps someone in a higher position; a subordinate who supports a leader or manager
  - 例：She works as an assistant to the CEO.（她是執行長的助理。）
  - 例：a teaching assistant（助教）、a research assistant（研究助理）
  - 補充：assistant to X（某人的助理）vs. assistant X（副某某，如 assistant manager 副經理）——兩者意思不同
- **dialogue:** I'm assistant to the Dean of the Academy.
  - Viktor 自報身分：我是學院院長的助理。用 "assistant to the Dean" 而不是隨便一個助理，強調自己直屬最高層

## dean
- **n.** the head of a college, faculty, or academic department; the highest-ranking administrator of an academic institution
  - 例：the Dean of Engineering（工學院院長）
  - 例：She was appointed dean last year.（她去年被任命為院長。）
  - 常見搭配：Dean of Students（學務長）、Dean of the Faculty（教務長）、Dean of the Academy（學院院長）
- **dialogue:** I'm assistant to the Dean of the Academy.
  - Arcane 中的 Dean 不只是學術職位——Viktor 緊接著提醒 Jayce，這位院長同時也是議會主席，是 Piltover 最有權力的人

## head
- **n.** the person in charge; the leader or chief of an organization or group
  - 例：She's the head of the department.（她是部門主管。）
  - 例：the head of state（國家元首）
  - 常見搭配：head of the Council（議會主席）、head of department（部門主管）、head of household（戶長）
- **dialogue:** ...is also head of the Council.
  - Viktor 補刀：院長同時也是議會主席——學術權力加政治權力，你惹的人是整個 Piltover 的最高權威

## send
- **v.** to order or arrange for someone to go somewhere for a purpose; closer to 中文的「派」than「寄」— implies authority and purpose, not just "tell someone to come"
  - 例：The company sent me to Tokyo for training.（公司派我去東京受訓。）
  - 例：My boss sent me here to fix the problem.（老闆派我來處理這個問題。）
  - 例：They sent a team to investigate.（他們派了一組人去調查。）
  - 常見搭配：send someone to do something（派某人去做某事）、send someone here/there（派某人到這裡/那裡）
- **dialogue:** He sent me here to ensure that anything dangerous is removed safely.
  - Viktor 說院長派他來確保危險物品被安全移除，sent 帶有受命差遣的語感

## according to
- **prep.** as stated or shown by; based on a source of information
  - 例：According to the report, sales are up 20%.（根據報告，銷售成長了 20%。）
  - 例：According to him, the meeting was cancelled.（照他的說法，會議取消了。）
  - 例：According to the rules, you can't park here.（按照規定，你不能停這裡。）
  - 常見搭配：according to the data（根據數據）、according to my records（根據我的紀錄）、according to plan（按照計劃）
- **dialogue:** Which, according to my list, includes you.
  - Viktor 說根據他的清單，需要移除的危險物品——包括 Jayce 本人。最後兩個字 "includes you" 才是真正的殺招

## imprisonment
- **n.** the state of being kept in prison; confinement as punishment
  - 例：He was sentenced to five years of imprisonment.（他被判處五年監禁。）
  - 例：life imprisonment（終身監禁）
  - 構詞：imprison（v. 監禁）+ -ment（名詞化）
- **dialogue:** Imprisonment. What a curious principle.
  - 漢默丁格初登場，走進牢房第一句不是問候，而是對「監禁」這個概念發表哲學感想——非常符合老學者的性格

## confine
- **v.** to keep someone or something within limits; to restrict to a particular place
  - 例：The prisoners were confined to their cells.（囚犯被關在牢房裡。）
  - 例：Please confine your remarks to the topic.（請把你的發言限制在主題範圍內。）
  - 常見搭配：confine to（限制在⋯）、be confined to bed（臥病在床）、confined space（密閉空間）
- **dialogue:** We confine the physical body, yet the mind is still free.
  - 漢默丁格的哲學觀察：我們能禁錮肉體，但心靈無法被關住

## conundrum
- **n.** a confusing or difficult problem; a paradox that seems to have no clear solution
  - 例：The budget is a real conundrum.（預算問題真是個難題。）
  - 例：It's a moral conundrum.（這是個道德難題。）
- **dialogue:** I do love a good conundrum.
  - 漢默丁格說自己就是喜歡好的悖論——監禁關得住身體卻關不住思想，這種矛盾讓他著迷

## scientist
- **n.** a person who studies or has expert knowledge in a natural or physical science
  - 例：She's a computer scientist.（她是電腦科學家。）
  - 例：Scientists discovered a new species.（科學家發現了新物種。）
  - 構詞：science（科學）+ -ist（從事⋯的人）
- **dialogue:** A scientist ready to forge a new vector of experimentation.
  - 漢默丁格形容初見 Jayce 時的印象——一個準備好開創新方向的科學家

## forge
- **v.** to create or develop something new with great effort; originally means to shape metal by heating and hammering
  - 例：They forged a new path through the jungle.（他們在叢林中開闢了一條新路。）
  - 例：She forged a career in politics.（她在政界闖出了一片天。）
  - 常見搭配：forge a path（開闢道路）、forge ahead（奮力前進）、forge an alliance（締結聯盟）
- **dialogue:** A scientist ready to forge a new vector of experimentation.
  - forge 在這裡是「開創」，帶有鍛造般用力打造的意象

## experimentation
- **n.** the process of conducting experiments; testing new ideas or methods
  - 例：Scientific experimentation requires patience.（科學實驗需要耐心。）
  - 例：The drug is still in the experimentation phase.（這款藥物還在實驗階段。）
  - 構詞：experiment（實驗）+ -ation（名詞化）
- **dialogue:** A new vector of experimentation.
  - 全新的實驗方向——漢默丁格用學術措辭讚美 Jayce 的研究野心

## venture
- **v.** to dare to go somewhere or do something risky or unknown; to boldly move forward
  - 例：Few people ventured into the forest at night.（很少人敢在晚上進入那片森林。）
  - 例：She ventured into the world of business.（她大膽踏入了商界。）
  - 常見搭配：venture into（冒險進入）、venture out（大膽外出）、venture too far（走得太遠）
- **dialogue:** But sometimes we venture too far.
  - 漢默丁格從讚美轉為警告——有時候我們冒險走得太遠了。用 "we" 把自己也包含在內，語氣溫和但意思明確

## manner
- **n.** a kind or type; a way of doing something — formal alternative to "kind" or "type"
  - 例：What manner of creature is this?（這是什麼樣的生物？）
  - 例：All manner of problems arose.（各式各樣的問題都冒出來了。）
  - 常見搭配：what manner of（什麼樣的）、all manner of（各種各樣的）、in a manner of speaking（某種意義上來說）
- **dialogue:** What manner of inquiry was this?
  - 漢默丁格問「這是什麼樣的研究」，用 manner 而不是 kind，非常學者風格

## inquiry
- **n.** a formal investigation or systematic study; also means a question or request for information
  - 例：The police launched an inquiry into the incident.（警方對事件展開調查。）
  - 例：scientific inquiry（科學探究）
  - 例：I'd like to make an inquiry about the job.（我想詢問一下這份工作的事。）
  - 常見搭配：conduct an inquiry（進行調查）、line of inquiry（調查方向）、inquiry into（針對⋯的調查）
- **dialogue:** What manner of inquiry was this?
  - 漢默丁格用 inquiry 而不是 research 或 experiment，帶有追問真相的語感——你這到底算哪門子的研究？

## harness
- **v.** to control and make use of a natural force or power; originally means to put a harness on a horse
  - 例：We can harness solar energy to power homes.（我們可以利用太陽能來供電。）
  - 例：The dam harnesses the power of the river.（水壩利用河流的力量。）
  - 例：She learned to harness her anger into motivation.（她學會把憤怒轉化為動力。）
  - 常見搭配：harness energy（利用能量）、harness the power of（駕馭⋯的力量）、harness technology（運用技術）
- **dialogue:** A way to harness magic through science.
  - Jayce 的核心野心——用科學駕馭魔法。harness 精準傳達了「把不受控的力量收為己用」的概念，也是整部 Arcane 的故事主軸。Piltover 崇尚科學，魔法是禁忌領域，Jayce 等於在對院長坦承自己在碰禁忌

## breakthrough
- **n.** an important discovery or development that removes a barrier to progress
  - 例：Scientists made a major breakthrough in cancer research.（科學家在癌症研究上取得重大突破。）
  - 例：The negotiations finally had a breakthrough.（談判終於有了突破。）
  - 常見搭配：a major breakthrough（重大突破）、close to a breakthrough（快要突破了）、make a breakthrough（取得突破）
- **dialogue:** I'm close to a breakthrough, I know it.
  - Jayce 堅信自己快要突破了。漢默丁格說魔法不可控，Jayce 不接受，用這句展現他的執著

## pursue
- **v.** to chase or follow something in order to catch or achieve it; to continue working toward a goal
  - 例：She decided to pursue a career in medicine.（她決定追求醫學事業。）
  - 例：He pursued his dream despite all obstacles.（他不顧一切阻礙追求夢想。）
  - 常見搭配：pursue a goal（追求目標）、pursue knowledge（追求知識）、pursue a career（追求事業）
- **dialogue:** All my life, I've pursued the mysteries of science.
  - 漢默丁格說自己一生都在追尋科學的奧秘——活了幾百年的人說這句話，份量很重

## own
- **v.** to admit or accept responsibility for something, especially a mistake; not "possess" but "take accountability"
  - 例：You need to own your mistakes.（你得承認你的錯。）
  - 例：He owned up to what he did.（他坦承了自己做的事。）
  - 常見搭配：own your mistakes（認下你的錯）、own up to（坦承）、own it（認了吧）
- **dialogue:** Own your mistakes before the Council.
  - 漢默丁格叫 Jayce 在議會面前認錯——先保命再說

## theorize
- **v.** to form a theory; to speculate based on reasoning — a scholarly way to say "I think"
  - 例：Scientists theorize that the universe is still expanding.（科學家推測宇宙仍在膨脹。）
  - 例：He theorized that the problem was in the code.（他推測問題出在程式碼。）
- **dialogue:** I theorize you'll get away with a slap on the wrist.
  - 漢默丁格連口語建議都用 theorize，一般人會說 "I think"，他偏要說「我推測」——學者本色

## dump
- **n.** (informal) a dirty, ugly, or unpleasant place
  - 例：This apartment is a dump.（這間公寓破到不行。）
  - 例：Why are we eating at this dump?（我們幹嘛在這種破地方吃飯？）
- **dialogue:** Remind me why we bother with this dump.
  - Mylo 嫌棄練習場地很破爛

## insist
- **v.** to say firmly that something is true, especially when others doubt it; to demand something strongly
  - 例：He insisted he was innocent.（他堅持說自己是無辜的。）
  - 例：She insisted on paying.（她堅持要付錢。）
  - 常見搭配：insist on（堅持要）、insist that（堅持說）
- **dialogue:** They insist business is steady.
  - Ferros 家族堅持說生意很穩定，Mel 聽了不買帳

## stagnant
- **adj.** not growing or developing; stuck in place — originally describes water that doesn't flow and becomes foul
  - 例：The economy has been stagnant for years.（經濟停滯了好幾年。）
  - 例：stagnant water breeds mosquitoes.（死水滋生蚊子。）
  - 常見搭配：stagnant economy（停滯的經濟）、stagnant growth（零成長）
- **dialogue:** Steady is stagnant, Elora.
  - Mel 一句話翻轉——穩定就是停滯。不成長就是在退步，展現她野心勃勃的性格

## poorest
- **adj.** superlative of "poor"; the least wealthy — here used in a relative sense within an extremely wealthy family
  - 例：He's the poorest kid in a rich school.（他是富人學校裡最窮的小孩。）
  - 例：It's the poorest region in the country.（這是全國最窮的地區。）
- **dialogue:** Yet I remain the poorest Medarda.
  - Mel 是 Piltover 最有錢的人，但在 Medarda 家族裡排最末——暗示家族勢力有多龐大，也透露她來 Piltover 的野心和壓力

## revolutionary
- **adj.** completely new and having a great effect; involving or causing a dramatic change
  - 例：The iPhone was a revolutionary product.（iPhone 是革命性的產品。）
  - 例：a revolutionary idea（革命性的想法）
  - 構詞：revolution（革命）+ -ary（形容詞化）
- **dialogue:** We need something revolutionary, Elora.
  - Mel 不要穩定，要的是能翻轉局面的東西——Jayce 的魔法科學研究即將進入她的視線

## trial
- **n.** a formal examination in a court of law to decide if someone is guilty; also means a test or experiment
  - 例：The trial begins next Monday.（審判下週一開始。）
  - 例：He's on trial for fraud.（他因詐欺被審判。）
  - 常見搭配：on trial（受審中）、stand trial（出庭受審）、trial and error（試誤法）
- **dialogue:** What of today's trial?
  - Mel 問今天 Jayce 的審判情況

## collapsible
- **adj.** able to be folded down or made smaller for easy storage or carrying
  - 例：a collapsible umbrella（折疊傘）
  - 例：a collapsible chair（折疊椅）
  - 構詞：collapse（折疊、塌陷）+ -ible（可⋯的）
- **dialogue:** The collapsible pocket wrench.
  - Talis 家族靠可折疊口袋扳手起家——Mel 聽了直接嗤之以鼻

## pocket wrench
- **n.** a small wrench designed to fit in a pocket; a portable hand tool
  - 例：He always carries a pocket wrench.（他隨身帶著口袋扳手。）
  - 補充：pocket 當形容詞 = 口袋大小的、隨身的，如 pocket knife（折疊刀）、pocket watch（懷錶）
- **dialogue:** Their design of the collapsible pocket wrench.
  - Talis 家族的成名之作，Elora 認真介紹但 Mel 完全不感興趣

## favor
- **v.** to prefer or support someone; to show special treatment or approval toward someone
  - 例：The teacher clearly favors her.（老師明顯偏心她。）
  - 例：Fortune favors the bold.（幸運偏愛勇者。）
  - 常見搭配：favor someone（偏愛某人）、in favor of（支持）、do someone a favor（幫某人一個忙）
- **dialogue:** But Heimerdinger favors him?
  - Mel 關注的不是 Jayce 的家世，而是漢默丁格看好他——這才是有價值的情報

## patron
- **n.** a person who financially supports an artist, scholar, or cause; a sponsor
  - 例：The museum relies on its patrons for funding.（博物館依靠贊助人提供資金。）
  - 例：She found a patron willing to fund her research.（她找到了一位願意資助她研究的贊助人。）
  - 常見搭配：patron of the arts（藝術贊助人）、patron saint（守護聖人）
- **dialogue:** We're his patrons.
  - Mrs. Kiramman 說 Kiramman 家一直在金援 Jayce 的研究，作為贊助人有責任替他說話

## rob
- **v.** to steal from a person or place by force or threat; the target is the victim, not the object taken
  - 例：He was robbed on the street.（他在街上被搶了。）
  - 例：They robbed a bank.（他們搶了銀行。）
  - 補充：rob a person/place（搶人/搶地方）；steal a thing（偷東西）。不能說 ~~rob my phone~~
- **dialogue:** He was robbed.
  - Caitlyn 替 Jayce 辯護——他是被搶的受害者，爆炸不是他的錯

## for years
- **adv.** for a long period of time; emphasizes duration
  - 例：I've known him for years.（我認識他好幾年了。）
  - 例：She's been studying piano for years.（她學鋼琴學了好幾年。）
  - 補充：同類用法有 for ages（好久）、for months（好幾個月）、for a long time（很長一段時間）
- **dialogue:** We've known Jayce for years.
  - Mrs. Kiramman 強調跟 Jayce 認識很久了，不是萍水之交

## allergic
- **adj.** having a physical reaction (rash, swelling, etc.) to a substance that is harmless to most people
  - 例：I'm allergic to peanuts.（我對花生過敏。）
  - 例：Are you allergic to anything?（你對什麼東西過敏嗎？）
  - 常見搭配：allergic to（對⋯過敏）、allergic reaction（過敏反應）
- **dialogue:** Don't you know I'm allergic?
  - 議員送漢默丁格銀杏果，結果他過敏——跟 Mel 精心挑選的禮物形成對比，突顯 Mel 的用心

## endanger
- **v.** to put someone or something at risk of harm or destruction
  - 例：Drunk driving endangers everyone on the road.（酒駕危害路上的所有人。）
  - 例：The oil spill endangered marine life.（漏油事件危害了海洋生物。）
  - 常見搭配：endanger lives（危害生命）、endangered species（瀕危物種）
- **dialogue:** Accused of endangering the citizens of Piltover.
  - Jayce 被控危害 Piltover 市民安全

## reckless
- **adj.** acting without thinking about the consequences; dangerously careless
  - 例：reckless driving（魯莽駕駛）
  - 例：It was reckless of him to jump off that wall.（他從牆上跳下來太莽撞了。）
  - 常見搭配：reckless behavior（魯莽行為）、reckless driving（危險駕駛）、reckless disregard（罔顧）
- **dialogue:** It was reckless, and for that, I'm sorry.
  - Jayce 認錯時用 reckless 形容自己的行為——魯莽、不顧後果

## forgiveness
- **n.** the act of pardoning someone for a mistake or wrongdoing
  - 例：He asked for her forgiveness.（他請求她的原諒。）
  - 例：Forgiveness doesn't mean forgetting.（原諒不等於遺忘。）
  - 構詞：forgive（原諒）+ -ness（名詞化）
- **dialogue:** I ask the Council's forgiveness.
  - Jayce 在審判中正式請求議會寬恕

## meaningless
- **adj.** having no purpose, value, or significance
  - 例：The data is meaningless without context.（沒有脈絡的話，這些數據毫無意義。）

## found

## cultivate

## ethos

## banish

## foolish

## unwise

  - 例：It would be unwise to ignore the warning signs.（忽視那些警訊是不明智的。）

## overlook

## sentence

## intrigue

- **dialogue:** I must admit, his theory intrigues.

## dispose of

  - 常見搭配：dispose of waste（處理廢棄物）、dispose of evidence（銷毀證據）、safely disposed of（安全銷毀）
- **dialogue:** His research will be safely disposed of.
  - 漢默丁格交代 Viktor：明天早上 Jayce 的研究材料就會被安全銷毀。用 disposed of 而非 thrown away，帶有「按程序處理危險物品」的正式語感

## misfit
- **n.** a person who doesn't fit in with a group or environment; someone who is out of place — 格格不入的人、不合群的怪咖
  - 例：He was always a misfit at school.（他在學校一直格格不入。）

  - 構詞：mis-（不、錯）+ fit（適合）= 不適合的人
- **dialogue:** My dad says you're a misfit.

## culprit
- **n.** a person who has committed a crime or done something wrong
  - 例：Police are still searching for the culprits.（警方仍在搜尋犯人。）
  - 例：The main culprit behind the pollution is the factory.（造成污染的元兇是那間工廠。）
  - 補充：比 criminal 更正式，常見於新聞、法律、官方語境。也可用於非犯罪情境，指「問題的根源」
- **dialogue:** The culprits must be apprehended.

## apprehend
- **v.** to arrest or capture someone (formal)
  - 例：The suspect was apprehended at the airport.（嫌犯在機場被逮捕。）
  - 補充：非常正式的用詞，一般口語用 catch / arrest。常見於官方聲明、新聞報導
- **dialogue:** The culprits must be apprehended.
  - 議員用 apprehended 而不是 caught 或 arrested，語氣正式而有距離感，符合上位者的身分

## exhaustive

## interrogation

## conduct

## drop

## arrogance

## bravery

## bury
