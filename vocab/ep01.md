# Episode 01

## bare
- **adj.** not covered; without clothing or decoration
  - 例：bare feet（赤腳）
- **adj.** empty; with nothing in it or on it
  - 例：The cupboard was bare.（櫥櫃是空的。）
- **adj.** only just enough; the minimum
  - 例：the bare essentials（最基本的必需品）
- **lyrics:** ♪ My hands are cold and bare ♪
  - 手空空的，什麼都沒有

## spare
- **adj.** extra; more than what is needed
  - 例：Do you have a spare pen?（你有多的筆嗎？）
- **v.** to give something you don't need to someone else
  - 例：Can you spare some change?（你能給一些零錢嗎？）
- **lyrics:** ♪ I'll take what you can spare ♪
  - 你能分給我的，我都收

## ask of
- **phrasal v.** a formal way of saying "ask someone for something"
  - 例：I ask a favor of you.（我請你幫個忙。）
- **lyrics:** ♪ I ask of you a penny ♪
  - 我向你要一分錢

## penny
- **n.** the smallest unit of currency; one cent
  - 例：I don't have a penny left.（我一分錢都不剩了。）
- **lyrics:** ♪ I ask of you a penny ♪
  - 只求最微薄的一點點

## fortune
- **n.** wealth; a large amount of money
  - 例：He made a fortune in real estate.（他靠房地產發了財。）
- **n.** luck; chance
  - 例：I had the good fortune to meet her.（我有幸遇見她。）
- **lyrics:** ♪ My fortune it will be ♪
  - 那一分錢就是我全部的財富了

## envy
- **n.** the feeling of wanting what someone else has
  - 例：She looked at his car with envy.（她羨慕地看著他的車。）
- **v.** to wish you had what someone else has
  - 例：I envy your courage.（我羨慕你的勇氣。）
- **lyrics:** ♪ I ask you without envy ♪
  - 我向你開口，但不帶嫉妒

## raise
- **v.** to build; to construct
  - 例：They raised a monument in the square.（他們在廣場上立了一座紀念碑。）
- **v.** to lift something up
  - 例：Raise your hand if you agree.（同意的話請舉手。）
- **lyrics:** ♪ We raise no mighty towers ♪
  - 我們蓋不起雄偉的高塔

## mighty
- **adj.** very strong and powerful; impressive
  - 例：the mighty ocean（壯闊的大海）
- **lyrics:** ♪ We raise no mighty towers ♪
  - 雄偉的高塔，屬於 Piltover 而非 Zaun

## gotta
- **informal** short for "got to" / "have to"; must
  - 例：I gotta go now.（我現在得走了。）
- **dialogue:** Gotta stay out of sight for this one.
  - 這次得避人耳目

## pull
- **v.** (informal) to carry out or execute something, especially a plan or scheme
  - 常見搭配：
    - pull a job — 執行一次行動（常指偷竊/犯罪）
    - pull a prank — 惡作劇
    - pull a stunt — 搞一個大膽的行動
    - pull it off — 成功做到某件難事
  - 例：They pulled a bank robbery last night.（他們昨晚搶了銀行。）
  - 例：I can't believe you pulled it off.（我不敢相信你竟然做到了。）
- **dialogue:** This is exactly the sort of job Vander would've pulled.
  - 這正是 Vander 會幹的那種事——帶有「幹一票」的味道

## screw up
- **phrasal v.** (informal) to make a mistake; to do something badly
  - 例：I really screwed up the exam.（我考試真的考砸了。）
  - 例：Don't screw this up for everyone.（別把大家的事搞砸了。）
- **dialogue:** Yeah, only if we screw up. / So don't screw up.
  - 只要不搞砸就沒事，Vi 用來安撫 Claggor 的擔心

## pick a lock
- **v.** to open a lock without a key by manipulating its internal mechanism with a tool
  - 例：He picked the lock in under a minute.（他不到一分鐘就把鎖撬開了。）
  - 相關詞：lockpick（開鎖工具）
- **dialogue:** Seeing as I'm the only one who knows how to pick locks, I suggest--
  - Mylo 強調只有自己會開鎖，暗示 Vi 應該對他客氣點

## trimmer
- **n.** a tool or device used for trimming (cutting/clipping something neat or short)
  - 例：a hair trimmer（理髮器）、a beard trimmer（修鬍器）、a hedge trimmer（修籬剪）
  - 構詞：trim（修剪）+ -er（工具）
- **dialogue:** That's a nose hair trimmer.
  - Mylo 得意地拿起一個東西問是啥，Powder 冷冷告訴他那只是鼻毛修剪器

## junk
- **n.** useless or worthless things
  - 例：The garage is full of junk.（車庫堆滿了廢物。）
  - 例：I need to throw out all this junk.（我得把這些破爛丟掉。）
- **dialogue:** Before Mylo fills the bag with junk.
  - Vi 損 Mylo 眼光差，怕他把袋子塞滿沒用的東西

## tip
- **n.** a piece of secret or inside information; a lead
  - 例：The police got a tip about the robbery.（警方收到了關於搶案的線報。）
  - 常見搭配：a tip from someone（來自某人的線報）、tip off / tip-off（通風報信）
- **dialogue:** It was a tip from Little Man.
  - Vi 說這個地點是 Little Man 給的線報

## penthouse
- **n.** a luxury apartment on the top floor of a building
  - 例：They live in a penthouse overlooking the city.（他們住在可以俯瞰城市的頂樓豪宅。）
- **dialogue:** We just emptied a Piltover penthouse right under the enforcers' noses.
  - Vi 說他們剛搬空了一間 Piltover 的頂樓豪宅

## haul
- **n.** (informal) a quantity of goods obtained, especially stolen goods or loot
  - 例：The thieves made off with a big haul.（小偷帶走了一大批贓物。）
  - 例：We got a good haul from the garage sale.（我們在車庫拍賣撈到不少好東西。）
  - 常見搭配：a nice haul（收穫不錯）、a big haul（大豐收）、make a haul（撈一票）
- **dialogue:** Nice haul?
  - 小混混看到 Vi 一行人帶著東西從上城區回來，試探性地問「撈到好貨了？」

## action
- **n.** (informal) exciting, risky, or illicit activity; things happening — often implies something not entirely legal or above-board
  - 例：Where's the action?（哪裡有搞頭？）
  - 例：He wanted a piece of the action.（他想分一杯羹。）
  - 例：Did you see any action last night?（昨晚有遇到什麼事嗎？）
  - 常見搭配：some action（一些動靜）、a piece of the action（分一杯羹）、where's the action?（哪裡有好玩的？）
- **dialogue:** I heard there was some action across the river.
  - Deckard 故意用輕鬆的語氣試探，暗示「我知道你們跑去上城搞事了」

## track
- **v.** to carry something (dirt, mud, trouble) from one place to another on your feet as you walk; to drag or trail something unwanted along with you
  - 例：Don't track mud into the house!（別把泥巴踩進屋裡！）
  - 例：You're tracking water all over the floor.（你把水踩得滿地都是。）
- **dialogue:** But now you're tracking this mess of yours through my streets.
  - Deckard 比喻 Vi 他們把上城惹的麻煩一路「踩」回他的地盤，所以他要收過路費

## vile
- **adj.** extremely unpleasant; disgusting
  - 例：The taste was absolutely vile.（味道噁心到不行。）
  - 例：What a vile thing to say.（這話說得真惡毒。）
- **dialogue:** This is vile.
  - 女方抽了一口 Vander 遞的煙斗後嗆到，嫌棄地說「這東西真難抽」——剛才還一副高高在上的姿態，結果一口底城菸就破功了

## foot chase
- **n.** a pursuit on foot; running after someone to catch them
  - 例：The police caught him after a foot chase through the alley.（警察在巷子裡追了一段後抓到他。）
  - 常見搭配：car chase（飛車追逐）、foot chase（徒步追逐）、high-speed chase（高速追逐）
- **dialogue:** I'm hearing about an explosion and a foot chase topside.
  - Vander 說他聽說上城區有爆炸和徒步追逐——指 Vi 他們被執法官追著跑的那段

## topside
- **adv.** on or to the upper area; in Arcane, refers to Piltover (the city above, as opposed to the undercity Zaun)
  - 例：Things are different topside.（上面的世界不一樣。）
  - 例：Nobody from topside cares about us.（上面的人不在乎我們。）
- **dialogue:** a foot chase topside
  - topside 在 Arcane 中是底城人對 Piltover 的說法——「上面」

## blow up
- **phrasal v.** to explode; to destroy something with an explosion
  - 例：The bomb blew up.（炸彈爆炸了。）
  - 例：They blew up the bridge.（他們炸掉了那座橋。）
  - 補充：口語中也可指手機被訊息轟炸（My phone is blowing up.）
- **dialogue:** You blew up a building.
  - Vi 說行動很順利沒人發現，Vander 一句「你們炸了一棟樓」直接打臉

## earn
- **v.** to gain something through effort or merit, not by luck or being given it
  - 例：You have to earn your place on this team.（你得靠實力爭取在這個隊伍的位置。）
  - 例：She earned their respect over time.（她隨著時間贏得了他們的尊重。）
  - 常見搭配：earn one's place（掙得一席之地）、earn respect（贏得尊重）、earn a living（謀生）、earn trust（贏得信任）
- **dialogue:** We have to earn our place in this world.
  - Vi 引用 Vander 平常說的話來反駁他——你自己教我們要靠自己爭取，我們就是在這麼做啊

## off-limits
- **adj.** forbidden to enter or use; not allowed — originally military terminology for areas soldiers cannot access
  - 例：The rooftop is off-limits to students.（學生禁止上頂樓。）
  - 例：That topic is off-limits.（那個話題不能碰。）
- **dialogue:** The Northside's off-limits.
  - Vander 回擊 Vi——你說要靠自己爭取沒錯，但我也一再說了北邊（上城區）是禁區，你們不准去

## plenty
- **pron.** a large amount; more than enough — used without a noun to mean "everything they need"
  - 例：Don't worry, we have plenty.（別擔心，我們很多。）
  - 例：They've got plenty while others have nothing.（他們什麼都有，別人卻一無所有。）
- **dialogue:** They've got plenty, while we're down here scraping together coins.
  - Vi 用 plenty（什麼都有）對比 coins（幾個硬幣），強調上城與底城的貧富差距

## selfish
- **adj.** caring only about yourself; not considering others
  - 例：Don't be so selfish — share with your sister.（別那麼自私——跟你妹分享。）
  - 例：It was selfish of me to leave without telling anyone.（我不告而別是很自私的。）
- **dialogue:** You don't get to be selfish.
  - Vander 不是在說 Vi 是自私的人，而是說當領袖的人連自私的權利都沒有——你的決定關係到其他人的安危

## informant
- **n.** a person who secretly provides information, especially to the police or someone in authority; a tipster
  - 例：The police relied on an informant to crack the case.（警方靠線人破了案。）
  - 例：He acted as an informant for the gang.（他當了幫派的線人。）
- **dialogue:** I'm gonna have a little word with your informant.
  - Vander 口中的 informant 指的是小艾克（Ekko），在 Benzo 店裡聽到情報後告訴 Vi 他們的人

## payout
- **n.** a sum of money paid out; a reward or return from a job or investment
  - 例：The lottery payout was $10 million.（樂透獎金一千萬。）
  - 例：The job was risky, but the payout was worth it.（這活很危險，但報酬值得。）
- **dialogue:** The biggest payout we've ever seen and she just lost it.
  - Mylo 抱怨 Powder 弄丟了他們見過最大的一筆收穫

## brag
- **v.** to boast; to talk proudly about yourself or your achievements in an annoying way
  - 例：He's always bragging about how much money he makes.（他老是在炫耀自己賺多少錢。）
  - 例：Stop bragging — nobody cares.（別吹了——沒人在意。）
- **dialogue:** And brag nonstop.
  - Vi 假裝在說 Powder 做不到的事，其實是在列 Mylo 的缺點——不停吹牛就是其中一項

## elsewhere
- **adv.** somewhere else; in or to another place
  - 例：If you're not happy here, go elsewhere.（你在這裡不開心就去別的地方。）
  - 例：The product is cheaper elsewhere.（這個東西在別的地方比較便宜。）
- **dialogue:** You can take your worthless junk elsewhere.
  - Benzo 嗆 Vander：你那些不值錢的破爛拿去別處吧

## busted
- **adj.** (informal) broken; not working
  - 例：My phone screen is busted.（我的手機螢幕壞了。）
  - 例：The lock's busted — we can't open it.（鎖壞了——打不開。）
- **dialogue:** The cannon pinion's still busted.
  - Ekko 說鐘錶零件還是壞的，還在修。從小就展現出搞機械的天分

## round up
- **phrasal v.** to gather or collect things/people together
  - 例：I'm still rounding up the numbers.（我還在彙整數字。）
  - 例：The teacher rounded up the students.（老師把學生集合起來。）
- **dialogue:** My guys are still roundin' up this month's collections.
  - Benzo 說手下還在收這個月的帳，還沒彙整完

## collections
- **n.** money collected regularly from various sources; periodic payments gathered from people
  - 例：The collections for this quarter are down.（這一季的收款減少了。）
  - 例：He's in charge of monthly collections.（他負責每月收款。）
- **dialogue:** This month's collections.
  - 底城各商家定期交給 Vander 的款項，Benzo 負責幫忙收齊

## bums
- **n.** (informal, derogatory) lazy or worthless people; also means homeless people or vagrants
  - 例：Some bums were hanging around the alley.（幾個流浪漢在巷子裡晃。）
  - 例：Don't be such a bum.（別這麼廢。）
- **dialogue:** Just some bums.
  - Claggor 用 bums 輕蔑地形容 Deckard 那群人——不是什麼執法官，只是一群小混混，不值一提

## otherwise
- **adv.** if not; if the situation were different — used to imply what would happen in the alternative scenario
  - 例：Wear a coat, otherwise you'll freeze.（穿外套，不然你會凍死。）
  - 例：She must be busy, otherwise she would have called.（她一定很忙，不然她會打電話來。）
- **dialogue:** I wouldn't be here otherwise.
  - Claggor 說 Vi 當然贏了，不然我也不會好好站在這跟你聊天了

## couple
- **n.** (informal) a few; a small number — strictly means "two" but in spoken English loosely means 2-3. Often used as "a couple of" or with "of" dropped in casual speech
  - 例：I'll be there in a couple minutes.（我幾分鐘就到。）
  - 例：Can I ask you a couple questions?（我能問你幾個問題嗎？）
  - 補充：a couple of + 名詞（標準）、a couple + 名詞（口語省略 of）
- **dialogue:** She showed me a couple moves to practice.
  - Ekko 說 Vi 教了他幾招讓他練，這裡省略了 of，口語用法

## weirdo
- **n.** (informal) a strange or eccentric person
  - 例：Some weirdo kept staring at me on the bus.（公車上有個怪人一直盯著我看。）
  - 例：Don't be such a weirdo.（別這麼怪。）
- **dialogue:** This weirdo came into the shop.
  - Ekko 形容一個行為反常的人來 Benzo 店裡——用黃金買展示品、不殺價，明顯不是底城的人

## haggle
- **v.** to negotiate or argue over a price; to bargain
  - 例：She haggled with the vendor for a lower price.（她跟攤販殺價。）
  - 例：I'm not going to haggle over a few dollars.（我不會為了幾塊錢討價還價。）
- **dialogue:** He paid in gold and didn't even haggle.
  - Ekko 說那個怪人連價都不殺，在底城這種地方不殺價非常反常，所以引起了他的懷疑

## sucker
- **n.** (informal) a fool; someone who is easily tricked or taken advantage of
  - 例：He paid $100 for that? What a sucker.（他花了100塊買那個？真是冤大頭。）
  - 例：There's a sucker born every minute.（每分鐘都有傻子誕生。）
- **dialogue:** Sucker.
  - Ekko 收了那個怪人雙倍價後得意地叫他冤大頭，展現底城小孩的精明

## district
- **n.** a defined area of a city or region, often with a specific purpose or character
  - 例：the business district（商業區）
  - 例：the shopping district（購物區）
  - 例：She lives in the historic district.（她住在歷史城區。）
- **dialogue:** The Academy district.
  - 指 Piltover 上城的學院區，Vi 他們炸掉的建築就在這一帶

## culprit
- **n.** (formal) a person who has committed a crime or is responsible for a problem
  - 例：The police are still searching for the culprits.（警方仍在搜尋犯人。）
  - 例：The culprit was caught on camera.（犯人被監視器拍到了。）
- **dialogue:** We're looking for the culprits.
  - 執法官的長官用正式用語說明來意——我們在找炸掉學院區建築的犯人

## picture
- **v.** to imagine or visualize something in your mind
  - 例：I can picture it now.（我現在能想像那個畫面。）
  - 例：Picture yourself on a beach.（想像你在海灘上。）
  - 例：Can you picture him as a teacher?（你能想像他當老師嗎？）
- **dialogue:** It's exactly who you're picturing in that thick head of yours.
  - 菜鳥執法官嗆 Vander：你腦袋裡想到的就是那個人，別裝了。用 "thick head"（笨腦袋）侮辱 Vander

## shady
- **adj.** (informal) suspicious; dishonest; not trustworthy
  - 例：That deal sounds pretty shady.（那筆交易聽起來很可疑。）
  - 例：He's a shady character.（他是個不正當的人。）
- **dialogue:** You shady son of a…
  - Marcus 氣到快爆粗口，罵 Vander 是「卑鄙的狗娘——」，話還沒說完就被長官攔下

## chap
- **n.** (British informal) a man or boy; similar to "guy"
  - 例：He's a nice chap.（他是個好人。）
  - 例：Who's that chap over there?（那邊那個傢伙是誰？）
- **dialogue:** Lovely chap you've brought.
  - Benzo 用反諷的語氣說「你帶來了個好傢伙啊」——諷刺 Marcus 態度惡劣
