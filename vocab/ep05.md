# Episode 05

## accusation
- **n.** a claim that someone has done something wrong or illegal — 指控、指責
  - 例：She made a serious accusation against her colleague.（她對同事提出嚴重指控。）
  - 例：He denied all accusations.（他否認了所有指控。）
  - 常見搭配：serious / false / unfounded accusation、make an accusation against sb、accusation of + n.
- **dialogue:** That's a serious accusation.
  - Grayson 沒有立刻否認，而是先點出這句話的份量——指控一個人收賄可不是小事

## deserve
- **v.** to be worthy of or have a right to something — 值得、應得
  - 例：You deserve better than this.（你值得比這更好的對待。）
  - 例：Don't apologize — you earned it.（別道歉，那是你應得的。）
  - 常見搭配：deserve credit（值得讚揚）、deserve a chance（應得一個機會）、deserve it（應得）
- **dialogue:** I just thought you deserved it.
  - Grayson 最終的解釋——不是被收買，而是認為 Caitlyn 本來就值得那個獎項

## handle
- **v.** to hold or operate a tool, weapon, or object — 操作、使用（工具、武器）
  - 例：Do you know how to handle a firearm?（你知道如何操作槍枝嗎？）
  - 例：He was trained to handle heavy machinery.（他受訓操作重型機械。）
  - 常見搭配：handle a weapon / tool / equipment、handle with care（小心操作）
  - 補充：handle 也可指「應對、處理」（handle a situation），但此處是字面的「操作」意義
- **dialogue:** For me, knowing how to handle this weapon means being able to protect people.
  - Grayson 說的 handle 是身為執法者必備的基本能力——會用槍不是為了贏比賽，而是為了保護人

## beg the question
- **idiom（口語）** to prompt or invite an obvious question; to raise a question naturally — 引出一個不得不問的問題
  - 例：His answer just begs the question: why did he lie?（他的回答讓人不禁想問：他為什麼說謊？）
  - 例：That success begs the question of whether the approach is scalable.（那個成功讓人不禁想問這方法能否擴大規模。）
  - 補充：哲學/邏輯上原指「循環論證」，但現代口語已普遍用來表示「引出一個顯而易見的問題」，兩種用法並存
- **dialogue:** Begs the question, young Kiramman. What are you shooting for?
  - Grayson 用這個詞引出核心問題——你練得這麼好，那你的目標究竟是什麼？

## inmate
- **n.** a person confined in a prison, hospital, or other institution — 囚犯、收容者（特指被關押／收容於某機構內的人）
  - 例：The inmates staged a riot.（囚犯們發動了暴動。）
  - 例：The hospital houses 300 inmates.（這間醫院收容了 300 名住院者。）
  - 區分：*prisoner* 泛指囚犯；*inmate* 強調「被收容於某機構內」，可指監獄或精神病院
- **dialogue:** You attacked an inmate. Why?
  - Caitlyn 質問 Vi 為何攻擊另一名獄友

## witness
- **n.** a person who sees an event happen or who gives evidence in court — 證人、目擊者
  - 例：The police are looking for witnesses.（警方正在尋找目擊者。）
  - 例：She was the key witness in the trial.（她是審判中的關鍵證人。）
  - 常見搭配：eyewitness（目擊者）、key witness（關鍵證人）、call a witness（傳喚證人）
- **dialogue:** He was a witness in an ongoing investigation.
  - Caitlyn 解釋 Vi 攻擊的那人是調查中的證人

## ongoing
- **adj.** continuing or still in progress — 進行中的、持續的
  - 例：This is part of an ongoing investigation.（這是一項進行中的調查的一部分。）
  - 例：There are ongoing discussions about the merger.（關於合併的討論仍在進行中。）
  - 常見搭配：ongoing investigation / ongoing process / ongoing debate / ongoing effort
- **dialogue:** He was a witness in an ongoing investigation.
  - 強調調查尚未結案——Vi 破壞的是正在進行的案件

## bummer
- **informal n.** an annoying or disappointing situation — 糟糕的事、令人失望的事
  - 例：It's a bummer that you can't come.（你不能來真是遺憾。）
  - 例：What a bummer! My flight got cancelled.（真衰！我的班機被取消了。）
  - 補充：口語感嘆詞，用來表達同情或失望。語氣可以是真心遺憾，也可以像 Vi 這裡——完全敷衍
- **dialogue:** Hmm, bummer.
  - Vi 毫無誠意的回應——「喔，真可惜」

## industrialist
- **n.** a person who owns or runs a large industrial company — 工業家、大企業經營者
  - 例：He was a wealthy industrialist.（他是個富有的工業家。）
  - 例：The industrialist donated millions to charity.（這位工業家捐了數百萬給慈善機構。）
  - 構詞：industry（工業）+ -alist（……者）
- **dialogue:** Silco? The industrialist?
  - Caitlyn 對 Silco 的認知——上城人眼中他只是個正當的工業家

## evidence vs proof
- **evidence n.** (uncountable) facts or signs that support a belief or claim — 證據（可能多項，不一定足以下結論）
  - 例：The police gathered evidence at the crime scene.（警方在犯罪現場蒐集證據。）
  - 注意：*a piece of evidence*（一項證據），不能說 *an evidence*
  - 常見搭配：solid evidence、gather / collect evidence、evidence of + n.
- **proof n.** evidence strong enough to establish truth — 足以證明的證據、確鑿證明
  - 例：Do you have proof that he stole it?（你有他偷的證據嗎？）
  - 常見搭配：proof of identity、living proof、beyond a shadow of proof
- **dialogue:** This is evidence. / I need proof if I'm to believe what you're saying about Silco.
  - 同一段對話中兩個詞並用——evidence 是「證據（還在查）」，proof 是「能讓我相信你的決定性證據」

## cooperative

## release

## Councilor vs counselor
- **Councilor n.** a member of a council (governing body) — 議員、議會成員
  - 例：She was elected as a city councilor.（她當選為市議員。）
- **counselor n.** a person who gives advice or guidance — 顧問、諮詢師、輔導員
  - 例：He's a grief counselor.（他是悲傷輔導師。）
  - 補充：發音相近但拼法、字義完全不同。Council = 議會；counsel = 建議／諮詢
- **dialogue:** I've got orders from Councilor Talis. / Since when's he a Councilor?
  - Jayce 剛升任議員，獄警還沒跟上這個消息

## control
- **v.** to have power or command over a person or situation — 掌控、駕馭（人或局面）
  - 例：No one can control Jinx — not even Silco.（沒人能控制 Jinx，連 Silco 都不行。）
  - 例：She struggled to control her anger.（她努力壓抑怒氣。）
  - 常見搭配：control a person / situation / crowd / temper
  - 補充：搭配人時帶有「能讓對方聽命行事」的意涵，比 manage 更強硬
- **dialogue:** You said you could control her.
  - Marcus 質問 Silco——你說你能掌控 Jinx，結果呢？

## ambush
- **v./n.** a surprise attack from a hidden position; to attack by surprise — 伏擊、埋伏襲擊
  - 例：The convoy was ambushed in the mountains.（車隊在山區遭到伏擊。）

  - 常見搭配：set up / lay an ambush、ambush sb / a shipment

## shipment
- **n.** a quantity of goods sent or transported together — 一批貨物、運送的貨

## culprit
- **n.** a person responsible for a crime or misdeed — 罪魁禍首、肇事者

  - 常見搭配：find / catch / identify the culprit、the real / main culprit
  - 補充：語體較正式；口語常用 the bad guy

## accomplish
- **v.** to successfully complete something significant — 達成、完成（重要的事）
  - 例：We've accomplished what no one thought possible.（我們完成了所有人都認為不可能的事。）
  - 例：She accomplished her goal in three years.（她三年內達成了目標。）
  - 常見搭配：accomplish a goal / mission / task / a great deal
  - 補充：比 finish / do 更正式，強調成就感與份量
- **dialogue:** We've accomplished a lot together, Marcus.
  - Silco 提醒 Marcus 兩人共謀已久，是綁定也是威脅

## achieve
- **v.** to successfully bring about a desired result — 達成、實現（目標、成就）
  - 例：He achieved his lifelong dream.（他實現了畢生的夢想。）
  - 例：There is much yet to achieve.（還有很多尚待完成。）
  - 常見搭配：achieve a goal / dream / success / recognition
  - 區分：*accomplish* 強調「完成」的過程感；*achieve* 強調「達到」的結果感，常與抽象成就搭配
- **dialogue:** And there is more yet to achieve.
  - Silco 用 achieve 接續 accomplish，刻意營造「我們在共創偉業」的修辭高度

## anonymous
- **adj.** not identified by name; of unknown identity — 匿名的、不具名的
  - 例：The tip came from an anonymous source.（這個線報來自匿名來源。）

## tidy
- **adj.** neat, orderly; (figuratively) suspiciously convenient — 整齊的；（引申）俐落得可疑
  - 例：Her desk is always tidy.（她的桌子總是很整齊。）

  - 補充：字面義是「整齊」，但形容方案／解釋時常帶**諷刺**意味，暗示「太剛好、像設計過的」

- **dialogue:** Oh, that's tidy for you.

## remain

  - 例：Despite the noise, she remained calm.（儘管吵雜，她仍保持冷靜。）

## fiasco
- **n.** a complete and humiliating failure — 徹底的失敗、出醜的慘敗事件
  - 例：The launch event turned into a total fiasco.（發表會變成一場大災難。）
  - 例：The negotiation ended in fiasco.（談判以慘敗收場。）
  - 常見搭配：a complete / total / embarrassing fiasco、end in fiasco
  - 補充：源自義大利文「酒瓶」，原指歌劇演出失敗，現泛指任何丟臉的失利
- **dialogue:** Yesterday's smuggling fiasco was nothing.
  - Jayce 形容昨天那場走私事件——而且暗示「後面更糟」

## manifest

  - 例：The customs officer checked the ship's manifest.（海關官員核對了船的艙單。）

  - 常見搭配：cargo / shipping / passenger manifest
  - 區分：作為形容詞時意為「明顯的」（manifest truth），動詞為「顯現」（manifest itself）——此處是名詞貨運單
- **dialogue:** These manifests are full of discrepancies, dating back months.

## discrepancy
- **n.** a difference between two things that should be the same — 不一致、差異、出入
  - 例：There's a discrepancy between the receipt and the bank statement.（收據和銀行對帳單對不上。）

  - 常見搭配：a discrepancy between A and B、notice / find / explain a discrepancy
  - 補充：常用於財務、紀錄、證詞等理應一致的內容出現落差
- **dialogue:** These manifests are full of discrepancies, dating back months.

## pledge

  - 常見搭配：make / break / honor / fulfill a pledge、pledge allegiance（宣誓效忠）
  - 補充：比 promise 更莊重，常用於公開承諾、宣誓場合
- **dialogue:** What about our pledge to improve lives, for those in need?

## assure / assurance（與 ensure / insure 區分）
- **assure v.** to tell someone confidently in order to remove doubt — 向某人保證、使放心
  - 例：I assure you that everything is under control.（我向你保證一切都在掌控中。）
  - 對象是**人**：assure sb (that ...)
- **assurance n.** a positive declaration intended to give confidence — 保證、把握
  - 例：He gave me his assurance that the work would be done on time.（他向我保證工作會準時完成。）
  - 常見搭配：give / receive / need an assurance
- **區分三兄弟：**
  - **assure**（讓人安心）→ I assure you it's safe.
  - **ensure**（確保某事發生）→ Please ensure the door is locked.
  - **insure**（投保）→ The car is insured against theft.
- **dialogue:** Mr. Talis, I assure you— / And the only assurance I need is that you will search and seize ...
  - Marcus 用 assure 想安撫，Jayce 立刻把它變名詞 assurance 反壓回去——對話技巧很高明

## merchandise
- **n.** (uncountable) goods that are bought and sold — 商品、貨物
  - 例：The store sells a wide range of merchandise.（這家店販售各式各樣的商品。）
  - 例：Customs seized the unauthorized merchandise.（海關扣押了未經授權的貨物。）
  - 常見搭配：unauthorized / counterfeit / official merchandise、display merchandise
  - 補充：**不可數**，不能說 *a merchandise*；複數形式請用 *goods*
- **dialogue:** ... you will search and seize any unauthorized merchandise.

## transaction
- **n.** a business deal or exchange — 交易、買賣
  - 例：The bank flagged several suspicious transactions.（銀行標出了幾筆可疑交易。）
  - 例：This transaction is final and non-refundable.（此交易為最終決定且不可退款。）
  - 常見搭配：suspicious / financial / cash transaction、conduct / complete a transaction
  - 衍生：*transactional*（adj. 交易性的）— 形容只談利益不談感情的關係
- **dialogue:** I've made a list of suspicious transactions.

## confer
- **v.** to discuss something with someone before making a decision — 商議、磋商、會商
  - 例：The judge conferred with both lawyers before ruling.（法官在裁決前與雙方律師進行了磋商。）
  - 例：Let me confer with my team and get back to you.（讓我跟團隊商量後再回覆你。）
  - 常見搭配：confer with sb (about / on sth)
  - 補充：比 *talk to* 正式，常用於決策、商務、法律情境；另一意思是「授予」（confer a degree 授予學位）
- **dialogue:** You sure you don't want to confer with the other Councilors before—
  - Marcus 暗勸 Jayce 先跟其他議員商量——典型的拖延／勸退話術

## corruption

  - 例：The new government promised to fight corruption.（新政府承諾打擊貪腐。）

  - 常見搭配：fight / root out / expose corruption、political / institutional corruption
  - 衍生：*corrupt*（adj. 腐敗的；v. 使腐敗）／*incorruptible*（adj. 廉潔不腐的）
- **dialogue:** This corruption runs deep, Sheriff. I intend to root it out.
  - Jayce 對 Marcus 宣示——要把貪腐連根拔起（諷刺的是 Marcus 本人就是貪腐源頭）

## phantom
- **n./adj.** a ghost; something that seems unreal or appears and disappears mysteriously — 幽靈、幻影；虛幻的
  - 例：He moved through the crowd like a phantom.（他像幽靈般穿過人群。）

## civilian

  - 例：Many civilians were caught in the crossfire.（許多平民被波及在交火中。）

  - 衍生：*civil*（adj. 公民的、民間的）／*civilization*（n. 文明）
- **dialogue:** ... doing hit-and-runs on civilians and disappearing into the night.

## stammer
- **v./n.** to speak with difficulty, repeating sounds or pausing nervously — 結巴、口吃地說
  - 例：He stammered an apology and rushed out.（他結結巴巴地道歉後便衝了出去。）
  - 例：She has a slight stammer when she's nervous.（她緊張時會有點結巴。）
  - 區分：*stammer*（多因緊張、情緒）／*stutter*（醫學上的口吃，較常用於先天性）— 兩者口語常混用
  - 補充：劇本中常出現於括號 *[stammers]*，表示角色因驚慌、被壓制、無言以對而結巴
- **dialogue:** [stammers] Right.
  - Marcus 被 Jayce 接連壓制後不知所措的反應——一個 stammers 把氣勢全寫出來
