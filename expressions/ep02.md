# Episode 02

## go over
- **phrasal v.** to review or examine something again carefully; commonly used in meetings, investigations, and study contexts
  - 例：Let's go over the plan one more time.（我們再把計劃過一遍。）
  - 例：Can you go over the details with me?（你可以跟我重新過一遍細節嗎？）
  - 常見搭配：go over it again（再過一遍）、go over the numbers（核對數字）、go over the facts（檢視事實）
- **dialogue:** Let's go over it again.
  - Grayson 的偵訊開場白——「我們再從頭過一遍」，看似耐心，實際上是在找漏洞

## should have + 過去分詞
- **sentence pattern** used to say something was the right thing to do, but it wasn't done; expresses regret, blame, or criticism about a past action (or inaction)
  - 例：You should have told me earlier.（你早該告訴我的。）
  - 例：I should have studied harder.（我當初應該更用功的。）
  - 例：We should have left before the rain started.（我們應該在下雨前就走的。）
  - 補充：口語中 should have 常縮讀成 should've，聽起來像 "should of"（但寫成 "should of" 是錯的）
- **dialogue:** I believe someone should have said that earlier.
  - Jayce 叫執法官小心別弄壞器材，Viktor 馬上接這句酸他——「我想這句話早該有人說了」。言下之意：你自己不小心處理才炸掉的，現在才說小心？用 "I believe" 包裝得彬彬有禮，實際上刻薄到不行，完美展現 Viktor 的毒舌性格

## Last time I checked, ...
- **sentence pattern** a sarcastic way to contradict someone by citing common knowledge or established facts; implies the other person is either lying or being ridiculous
  - 例：Last time I checked, you don't own this place.（據我所知，這地方不是你的吧。）
  - 例：Last time I checked, 2 + 2 was still 4.（我印象中 2 + 2 還是等於 4 吧。）
  - 例：Last time I checked, you were on my side.（我記得你本來是站在我這邊的吧。）
  - 補充：語氣等同於「我沒記錯的話⋯⋯」，但帶著「你少來」的意味
- **dialogue:** Last time I checked, science didn't require illegal equipment.
  - Jayce 用「科學實驗」輕描淡寫帶過，Grayson 直接用這句打臉——據我所知做科學不需要違法器材吧？把 Jayce 的藉口堵死

## Nor + 倒裝
- **sentence pattern** used after a negative statement to add another negative; requires inverted word order (verb before subject) — more formal and forceful than "and ... not ... either"
  - 例：I don't like it, nor does she.（我不喜歡，她也不。）
  - 例：He didn't call, nor did he text.（他沒打電話，也沒傳訊息。）
  - 例：She wasn't invited, nor was she welcome.（她沒被邀請，也不受歡迎。）
  - 補充：nor 後面一定要倒裝（助動詞/be 動詞提到主詞前面）。同樣的意思用 "either" 就不用倒裝：This wasn't approved by the Academy, either.
- **dialogue:** Nor was this approved by the Academy.
  - 前一句 Grayson 說器材違法，Viktor 用 nor 追加第二刀——學院也沒批准你的研究。連續兩個否定把 Jayce 的立場徹底擊垮：東西違法、研究也沒人授權，你什麼都站不住腳

## it may serve you to...
- **sentence pattern** a formal, condescending way to say "it would be in your best interest to..."; wraps a warning or threat in polite language
  - 例：It may serve you to remember who signs your paycheck.（你最好記清楚誰發你薪水的。）
  - 例：It may serve you to think before you speak.（說話之前想一想，對你比較好。）
  - 補充：serve 在這裡不是「服務」，而是「對⋯有利、有好處」
- **dialogue:** ...who it may serve you to remember is also head of the Council.
  - Viktor 把威脅包裝成好意提醒——「記住這件事對你比較好喔」。表面客氣，實際上在說：你最好搞清楚你在跟誰的人說話

## That's for someone to...
- **sentence pattern** used to say it's a specific person's job or right to do something; "for + 某人 + to V" = 由某人來做
  - 例：That's for you to decide.（那由你來決定。）
  - 例：It's not for me to say.（那不是我該說的。）
  - 例：That's for the judge to determine.（那由法官來判定。）
  - 例：It's not for us to question.（那不是我們該質疑的。）
- **dialogue:** That's for the council to decide.
  - Jayce 問自己哪裡危險，Viktor 一句「那由議會決定」把球踢走——我只負責執行，你的命運不在我手上

## yet
- **conjunction** but; however — used to introduce a contrast that is surprising or contradictory; more literary and dramatic than "but"
  - 例：He's rich, yet unhappy.（他很有錢，卻不快樂。）
  - 例：She was tired, yet she kept going.（她很累，卻繼續走下去。）
  - 例：It's simple, yet effective.（簡單卻有效。）
  - 補充：yet 當連接詞比 but 更有張力，常用於對比兩個矛盾的事實
- **dialogue:** We confine the physical body, yet the mind is still free.
  - 漢默丁格用 yet 帶出矛盾：肉體被禁錮，**然而**心靈依然自由。用 but 也通，但 yet 更有哲學味
