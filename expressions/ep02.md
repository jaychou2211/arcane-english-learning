# Episode 02

## go over
- **phrasal v.** to review or examine something again carefully; commonly used in meetings, investigations, and study contexts
  - 例：Let's go over the plan one more time.（我們再把計劃過一遍。）
  - 例：Can you go over the details with me?（你可以跟我重新過一遍細節嗎？）
  - 常見搭配：go over it again（再過一遍）、go over the numbers（核對數字）、go over the facts（檢視事實）
- **dialogue:** Let's go over it again.
  - Grayson 的偵訊開場白——「我們再從頭過一遍」，看似耐心，實際上是在找漏洞

## should have + 過去分詞
- **sentence pattern** used to say something was the right thing to do, but it wasn't done; expresses regret, blame, or criticism about a past action (or inaction)
  - 例：You should have told me earlier.（你早該告訴我的。）
  - 例：I should have studied harder.（我當初應該更用功的。）
  - 例：We should have left before the rain started.（我們應該在下雨前就走的。）
  - 補充：口語中 should have 常縮讀成 should've，聽起來像 "should of"（但寫成 "should of" 是錯的）
- **dialogue:** I believe someone should have said that earlier.
  - Jayce 叫執法官小心別弄壞器材，Viktor 馬上接這句酸他——「我想這句話早該有人說了」。言下之意：你自己不小心處理才炸掉的，現在才說小心？用 "I believe" 包裝得彬彬有禮，實際上刻薄到不行，完美展現 Viktor 的毒舌性格

## Last time I checked, ...
- **sentence pattern** a sarcastic way to contradict someone by citing common knowledge or established facts; implies the other person is either lying or being ridiculous
  - 例：Last time I checked, you don't own this place.（據我所知，這地方不是你的吧。）
  - 例：Last time I checked, 2 + 2 was still 4.（我印象中 2 + 2 還是等於 4 吧。）
  - 例：Last time I checked, you were on my side.（我記得你本來是站在我這邊的吧。）
  - 補充：語氣等同於「我沒記錯的話⋯⋯」，但帶著「你少來」的意味
- **dialogue:** Last time I checked, science didn't require illegal equipment.
  - Jayce 用「科學實驗」輕描淡寫帶過，Grayson 直接用這句打臉——據我所知做科學不需要違法器材吧？把 Jayce 的藉口堵死

## Nor + 倒裝
- **sentence pattern** used after a negative statement to add another negative; requires inverted word order (verb before subject) — more formal and forceful than "and ... not ... either"
  - 例：I don't like it, nor does she.（我不喜歡，她也不。）
  - 例：He didn't call, nor did he text.（他沒打電話，也沒傳訊息。）
  - 例：She wasn't invited, nor was she welcome.（她沒被邀請，也不受歡迎。）
  - 補充：nor 後面一定要倒裝（助動詞/be 動詞提到主詞前面）。同樣的意思用 "either" 就不用倒裝：This wasn't approved by the Academy, either.
- **dialogue:** Nor was this approved by the Academy.
  - 前一句 Grayson 說器材違法，Viktor 用 nor 追加第二刀——學院也沒批准你的研究。連續兩個否定把 Jayce 的立場徹底擊垮：東西違法、研究也沒人授權，你什麼都站不住腳

## it may serve you to...
- **sentence pattern** a formal, condescending way to say "it would be in your best interest to..."; wraps a warning or threat in polite language
  - 例：It may serve you to remember who signs your paycheck.（你最好記清楚誰發你薪水的。）
  - 例：It may serve you to think before you speak.（說話之前想一想，對你比較好。）
  - 補充：serve 在這裡不是「服務」，而是「對⋯有利、有好處」
- **dialogue:** ...who it may serve you to remember is also head of the Council.
  - Viktor 把威脅包裝成好意提醒——「記住這件事對你比較好喔」。表面客氣，實際上在說：你最好搞清楚你在跟誰的人說話

## That's for someone to...
- **sentence pattern** used to say it's a specific person's job or right to do something; "for + 某人 + to V" = 由某人來做
  - 例：That's for you to decide.（那由你來決定。）
  - 例：It's not for me to say.（那不是我該說的。）
  - 例：That's for the judge to determine.（那由法官來判定。）
  - 例：It's not for us to question.（那不是我們該質疑的。）
- **dialogue:** That's for the council to decide.
  - Jayce 問自己哪裡危險，Viktor 一句「那由議會決定」把球踢走——我只負責執行，你的命運不在我手上

## yet
- **conjunction** but; however — used to introduce a contrast that is surprising or contradictory; more literary and dramatic than "but"
  - 例：He's rich, yet unhappy.（他很有錢，卻不快樂。）
  - 例：She was tired, yet she kept going.（她很累，卻繼續走下去。）
  - 例：It's simple, yet effective.（簡單卻有效。）
  - 補充：yet 當連接詞比 but 更有張力，常用於對比兩個矛盾的事實
- **dialogue:** We confine the physical body, yet the mind is still free.
  - 漢默丁格用 yet 帶出矛盾：肉體被禁錮，**然而**心靈依然自由。用 but 也通，但 yet 更有哲學味

## remind someone of
- **phrasal v.** to cause someone to think of something or someone else; to trigger a memory or association
  - 例：This song reminds me of high school.（這首歌讓我想起高中。）
  - 例：You remind me of your father.（你讓我想起你爸。）
  - 例：That smell reminds me of home.（那個味道讓我想起家。）
  - 補充：remind of = 讓人「聯想到」；remind to = 提醒某人「去做」某事，兩者不同
- **dialogue:** You reminded me of myself.
  - 漢默丁格說 Jayce 讓他想起年輕時的自己——同樣充滿野心、準備開創新局

## I'm close to...
- **sentence pattern** almost reaching a goal or result; "close to + noun/V-ing" = 快要達到⋯
  - 例：I'm close to finishing the project.（我快要完成這個專案了。）
  - 例：We're close to a deal.（我們快要談成了。）
  - 例：She's close to tears.（她快要哭了。）
  - 常見搭配：close to a breakthrough（快要突破）、close to finishing（快要完成）、close to giving up（快要放棄）
- **dialogue:** I'm close to a breakthrough, I know it.
  - Jayce 被全面否定仍不退讓——我快要成功了，我知道。簡短但堅定

## only to
- **sentence pattern** used to introduce an unexpected or disappointing result after effort; "做了⋯結果卻⋯"
  - 例：I rushed to the station, only to find the train had left.（我趕到車站，結果發現火車已經開走了。）
  - 例：He studied all night, only to fail the exam.（他讀了一整夜，結果還是考砸了。）
  - 例：She called him, only to be ignored.（她打給他，結果被無視。）
- **dialogue:** All my life, I've pursued the mysteries of science, only to discover some are better left unsolved.
  - 追尋了一輩子，結果卻發現有些謎題不解開才是最好的——only to 帶出漫長努力後的醒悟

## better left + 過去分詞
- **sentence pattern** it's better to leave something in its current state; not touching it is the wiser choice
  - 例：Some things are better left unsaid.（有些話不說比較好。）
  - 例：That issue is better left alone.（那件事不去碰比較好。）
  - 例：The past is better left forgotten.（過去忘了比較好。）
- **dialogue:** Some are better left unsolved.
  - 有些謎題不解開反而比較好——漢默丁格幾百年的教訓

## I'm afraid...
- **sentence pattern** a polite way to deliver bad news or an unwelcome truth; NOT "I'm scared" but "unfortunately" or "恐怕"
  - 例：I'm afraid we're out of time.（恐怕我們沒時間了。）
  - 例：I'm afraid I can't help you.（恐怕我幫不了你。）
  - 例：I'm afraid that's not possible.（恐怕那不可能。）
- **dialogue:** This, I'm afraid, is one of them.
  - 漢默丁格用 I'm afraid 插在句中，語氣溫和但結論殘酷——恐怕你研究的魔法，就是不該碰的那種

## get away with
- **phrasal v.** to do something wrong and not get punished for it; to escape consequences
  - 例：He cheated on the test and got away with it.（他考試作弊卻沒被抓到。）
  - 例：You can't keep getting away with this.（你不能一直這樣僥倖下去。）
  - 例：She got away with just a warning.（她只被警告一下就沒事了。）
- **dialogue:** I theorize you'll get away with a slap on the wrist.
  - 漢默丁格說照他的建議做，Jayce 可以僥倖只受輕罰

## a slap on the wrist
- **idiom** a very mild punishment that doesn't really hurt; like an adult lightly tapping a child's hand
  - 例：He stole company funds and only got a slap on the wrist.（他偷了公司的錢，結果只被輕輕罰了一下。）
  - 例：A fine that small is just a slap on the wrist.（那麼少的罰款根本不痛不癢。）
  - 例：The judge gave him a slap on the wrist.（法官只輕判了他。）
- **dialogue:** You'll get away with, um, how do you say, a slap on the wrist.
  - 漢默丁格說完還故意停頓 "um, how do you say"——這種口語俚語對活了幾百年的學者來說太通俗了，得想一下才說得出來

## bother with
- **phrasal v.** to make the effort to deal with something; often used in negatives or questions to imply it's not worth the trouble
  - 例：Don't bother with the dishes, I'll do them later.（碗盤不用管了，我等等洗。）
  - 例：Why bother with cooking? Let's just order pizza.（何必費心煮飯？叫披薩就好了。）
  - 例：I wouldn't bother with that app — it's terrible.（那個 app 不值得費心——爛透了。）
- **dialogue:** Remind me why we bother with this dump.
  - Mylo 嫌棄地問：提醒我一下我們幹嘛費心窩在這種破地方

## as good a place as any
- **idiom** not the best, but acceptable; no better option exists — used to justify a mediocre choice
  - 例：It's not fancy, but it's as good a place as any.（不怎麼高級，但也沒有更好的了。）
  - 例：This spot is as good as any for a picnic.（這個地方野餐也行，反正都差不多。）

- **dialogue:** This is as good a place as any.

## it's thanks to X that Y
- **sentence pattern** an emphasis structure meaning "it's because of X that Y happened"; highlights the cause — can be grateful or sarcastic
  - 例：It's thanks to you that we won.（是因為你我們才贏的。）

## put something on the map
- **idiom** to make something famous or widely known; to bring recognition to a place, person, or thing

## What of...?

## As far as I can tell.

## blow over

## far more... than

  - Jayce 照漢默丁格的劇本認錯——承認違規，但不提魔法

## speak for someone's character
- **idiom** to vouch for someone's personality and integrity; to testify that someone is a good person
  - 例：I've known her for years — I can speak for her character.（我認識她很多年了——我能替她的人品擔保。）
  - 例：His colleagues spoke for his character at the hearing.（他的同事在聽證會上替他的品格作證。）
- **dialogue:** As Jayce's patron of many years, I can speak for his character.
  - Mrs. Kiramman 以多年贊助人的身分替 Jayce 的人品背書

## You can't make X without breaking a few Y.
- **sentence pattern** adapted from the proverb "you can't make an omelette without breaking a few eggs" — meaning you can't achieve something without some cost or collateral damage
  - 例：You can't make an omelette without breaking a few eggs.（要做蛋捲就得打幾顆蛋——要成事就免不了一些犧牲。）
  - 例：You can't build a company without making a few enemies.（要建立公司就免不了得罪幾個人。）
- **dialogue:** You can't make a prototype without breaking a few wrenches.
  - 漢默丁格把這句俗語改成科學家版本——做原型不可能不弄壞幾把扳手。替 Jayce 辯護的同時暗酸議員不懂科學，還故意用 wrench 呼應 Talis 家族的「口袋扳手」

## If I/you/he were..., I/you/he would...
- **sentence pattern** subjunctive mood for hypotheticals contrary to fact; always use "were" regardless of subject, paired with "would + V"
  - 例：If I were you, I'd apologize.（如果我是你，我會道歉。）
  - 例：If she were here, she'd agree.（如果她在這裡，她會同意。）
  - 例：If he were smarter, he wouldn't have said that.（如果他聰明一點，就不會那樣說了。）
  - 補充：用 were 而非 was，因為預設的情境跟事實相反
- **dialogue:** If you were a scientist, you'd know you can't make a prototype without breaking a few wrenches.
  - 漢默丁格直接預設「你不是科學家」再講道理——先否定你的資格，再反駁你的論點

## ___ how?
- **sentence pattern** a sharp follow-up that demands specifics; repeats the other person's claim and challenges them to back it up
  - 例："This will change everything." — "Change everything how?"（「這會改變一切。」——「怎麼個改變法？」）
  - 例："I'm different." — "Different how?"（「我不一樣。」——「哪裡不一樣？」）
- **dialogue:** Revolutionary how?
  - Mel 接住 Jayce 衝動喊出的 "revolutionary"，馬上追問——怎麼個革命法？繼續引導他講出研究內容

## meddle with
- **phrasal v.** to interfere with or tamper with something you shouldn't; implies incompetence or overstepping
  - 例：Don't meddle with things you don't understand.（別亂碰你不懂的東西。）
  - 例：Someone's been meddling with my computer.（有人動過我的電腦。）
  - 例：Stop meddling in other people's affairs.（別插手別人的事。）
  - 補充：meddle with（亂動東西）、meddle in（干涉事務）
- **dialogue:** All I see is a boy meddling with things he doesn't understand.
  - 議員貶低 Jayce——只是個小鬼在亂搞他不懂的東西

## loosen one's standards
- **idiom** to lower or relax the criteria or requirements; to become less strict
  - 例：The company has loosened its hiring standards.（公司放寬了招聘標準。）
  - 例：We can't afford to loosen our standards.（我們不能放鬆標準。）
- **dialogue:** The Academy seems to have loosened its standards.
  - 議員暗酸學院門檻降低了，才會讓 Jayce 這種人進來

## a fine line
- **idiom** a very small or subtle distinction between two things, often between acceptable and unacceptable
  - 例：There's a fine line between confidence and arrogance.（自信和自大之間只有一線之隔。）
  - 例：There's a fine line between genius and madness.（天才跟瘋子只有一線之隔。）
  - 例：We're walking a fine line here.（我們正在走鋼索。）
- **dialogue:** This is a fine line. If we condone this, what's next?
  - 議員認為這是很微妙的界線——一旦容忍就會開先例

## something you're born with
- **sentence pattern** an innate quality or trait that cannot be learned or acquired; "born with" = 與生俱來
  - 例：Talent is something you're born with.（天賦是天生的。）
  - 例：Confidence isn't something you're born with — you build it.（自信不是天生的——是培養的。）
  - 例：Some people are just born with it.（有些人就是天生就有。）
- **dialogue:** Arcane talents are something you're born with.
  - 議員認為魔法天賦是與生俱來的，不可能被人工製造

## champions of discovery
- **idiom** leaders or advocates in the pursuit of new knowledge; people who push the boundaries of what is known
  - 例：As champions of discovery, we should embrace the unknown.（身為探索的先驅，我們應該擁抱未知。）
  - 補充：champion 在這裡不是「冠軍」，而是「倡導者、擁護者」
- **dialogue:** We're the champions of discovery. Why fear it when we can master it?
  - Jayce 用激昂的措辭激勵議會——我們是探索未知的領頭羊，不該害怕

## master
- **v.** to gain complete control or understanding of something; to become highly skilled at
  - 例：It takes years to master a language.（精通一門語言需要好幾年。）
  - 例：She mastered the piano by age 15.（她 15 歲就精通鋼琴了。）
  - 例：He wants to master the art of negotiation.（他想精通談判的藝術。）
- **dialogue:** Why fear it when we can master it?
  - Jayce 的核心主張：我們可以征服魔法，何必害怕它

## what's at stake
- **idiom** what is being risked; what could be gained or lost depending on the outcome
  - 例：Do you understand what's at stake here?（你懂這裡面的利害關係嗎？）
  - 例：Millions of dollars are at stake.（好幾百萬美元正在被賭上。）
  - 例：Lives are at stake.（人命關天。）
  - 常見搭配：what's at stake（賭上的是什麼）、a lot is at stake（事關重大）、lives at stake（攸關性命）
- **dialogue:** You don't understand what's at stake.
  - 漢默丁格動怒——你不懂現在賭上的是什麼。他見過魔法毀滅文明，Jayce 沒有

## carry weight
- **idiom** to have influence or importance; 有份量、有影響力
  - 例：Her opinion carries a lot of weight in this company.（她的意見在這家公司很有份量。）
  - 例：His words don't carry much weight with me.（他的話對我沒什麼影響力。）
  - 常見搭配：carry much/little/no weight、carry weight with someone
- **dialogue:** As a lower house, my voice doesn't carry much weight here.
  - Ximena 自知家族地位低，在議會說話沒份量，但仍以母親身份為 Jayce 求情

## not in one's right mind
- **idiom** not thinking clearly; mentally or emotionally impaired; 神智不清、不在正常狀態
  - 例：He wasn't in his right mind when he made that decision.（他做那個決定的時候神智不清。）
  - 例：You must not be in your right mind to agree to that.（你答應那個一定是腦子不清楚。）
- **dialogue:** My son isn't in his right mind.
  - Ximena 替 Jayce 開脫——他做出那些事不是因為壞心，是因為被夢想沖昏了頭

## chase a dream
- **collocation** to pursue a dream or ambition, often implying difficulty or impracticality; 追夢
  - 例：She left her hometown to chase a dream of becoming an actress.（她離開家鄉去追逐當演員的夢想。）
  - 常見搭配：chase an impossible dream、chase one's dream
- **dialogue:** His entire life, he's chased an impossible dream.
  - Ximena 說 Jayce 一輩子都在追一個不可能的夢——研究魔法

## call for
- **phrasal v.** to require or demand; 要求、需要
  - 例：The situation calls for immediate action.（情況需要立即行動。）
  - 例：This calls for a celebration!（這值得慶祝一下！）
  - 補充：也可以是「呼籲」——They called for reform.（他們呼籲改革。）
- **dialogue:** A violation of the Ethos calls for banishment.
  - 違反 Ethos 按規定「要求」施以放逐——call for 在這裡表示規章制度上的要求

## sympathize with
- **phrasal v.** to feel or express understanding and compassion for someone's situation; 同情、理解
  - 例：I sympathize with your situation.（我同情你的處境。）
  - 例：It's hard not to sympathize with her.（很難不同情她。）
  - 補充：sympathy（同情心）、sympathetic（表示同情的）
- **dialogue:** I can sympathize with a young man's dream to change the world.
  - 漢默丁格同情 Jayce 的理想，為減刑鋪路

## I move that...
- **sentence pattern** formal parliamentary language for making an official proposal; 我動議⋯。後面接 subjunctive（動詞原形）
  - 例：I move that the meeting be adjourned.（我提議散會。）
  - 例：I move that we table this discussion.（我提議擱置這項討論。）
  - 補充：second the motion（附議）、the motion carries（動議通過）
- **dialogue:** I move that Jayce be summarily expelled from the Academy.
  - 漢默丁格正式提出動議——立刻開除 Jayce 學籍

## expelled from
- **phrasal v.** officially removed or dismissed from an institution; 被開除、被驅逐
  - 例：Two students were expelled from school for fighting.（兩名學生因打架被學校開除。）
  - 例：He was expelled from the party.（他被開除黨籍。）
  - 常見搭配：expelled from school/university/the party
- **dialogue:** Jayce be summarily expelled from the Academy.
  - 漢默丁格提議的刑罰——從學院開除，比放逐出城輕得多

## remanded to the care of
- **legal expression** officially placed under someone's supervision or custody; 交由⋯監管
  - 例：The juvenile was remanded to the care of his guardian.（該未成年人被交由監護人監管。）
  - 例：She was remanded to the custody of her parents.（她被交由父母監管。）
  - 補充：remand 是法律用語，常見於法庭判決
- **dialogue:** Remanded to the care of his parents.
  - 漢默丁格判決的第二部分——交由父母監管，等於讓 Jayce 回家

## All those in favor?
- **sentence pattern** formal call for a vote in a council or assembly; 贊成的請舉手？
  - 例：All those in favor, say aye.（贊成的請說 aye。）
  - 例：All those in favor? Motion carries.（贊成的？動議通過。）
  - 補充：反面——All those opposed?（反對的？）
- **dialogue:** All those in favor?
  - 漢默丁格提出動議後進行表決——議會程序的標準用語
