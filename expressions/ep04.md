# Episode 04

## shape up to be
- **phrasal v.** to develop or progress in a particular way; to gradually become something — 逐漸成形、看起來會變成
  - 例：The project is shaping up to be a success.（這個專案看起來會很成功。）
  - 例：How's the new team shaping up?（新團隊組得怎麼樣了？）
  - 例：The weather is shaping up to be nice this weekend.（這週末天氣看起來會不錯。）
  - 常見搭配：shape up to be...（看起來會成為⋯）、shape up nicely（發展得不錯）
- **dialogue:** It's shaping up to be an amazing Progress Day.
  - Heimerdinger 語氣期待——進展日看起來會非常精彩。用 shaping up 帶有「正在慢慢成形」的動態感

## and yet
- **conj.** nevertheless; despite what was just said — 然而、儘管如此。比 but 語氣更重，刻意強調前後的反差
  - 例：He failed countless times. And yet, he never gave up.（他失敗了無數次。然而，他從未放棄。）
  - 例：She had every reason to leave. And yet, she stayed.（她有所有理由離開。然而，她留下了。）
  - 補充：and yet 常放在句首，獨立成一個轉折，讓聽者先消化前面的資訊再被翻轉
- **dialogue:** And yet, here he stands.
  - Heimerdinger 先鋪墊 Stanwick 做什麼都失敗，再用 "And yet" 一句話翻轉——但他的雕像立在這裡。失敗不是定義一個人的唯一標準

## set aside
- **phrasal v.** to put something to one side; to deliberately choose not to pursue or focus on something — 擱置、放下
  - 例：Let's set aside our differences and work together.（我們放下歧見，一起合作吧。）
  - 例：She set aside some money for emergencies.（她存了一些錢以備不時之需。）
  - 例：He set aside his pride and apologized.（他放下驕傲，道了歉。）
  - 常見搭配：set aside differences（放下歧見）、set aside time / money（撥出時間／錢）、set aside personal feelings（放下個人情感）
- **dialogue:** Stanwick set his personal ambitions aside and focused on something far more important.
  - Stanwick 主動放棄個人成就，轉而培養學生。Heimerdinger 講這段不只是在追憶老友，更是在暗示 Jayce——真正的偉大是懂得放下自我

## take someone's place
- **idiom** to replace or substitute for someone; to fill the role that someone else held — 取代某人的位置
  - 例：No one can take her place.（沒有人能取代她。）
  - 例：Who's going to take his place when he retires?（他退休後誰來接他的位子？）
  - 補充：帶有情感份量——不只是「接替職位」，更暗示那個人不可替代
- **dialogue:** I could never take your place.
  - Jayce 的謙虛回應——進展日演講一直是 Heimerdinger 的，他覺得自己不配取代。但 Heimerdinger 正是刻意要交棒

## do wonders (for)
- **idiom** to have a surprisingly good effect; to bring about great improvement — 產生奇效、帶來巨大改善
  - 例：A good night's sleep can do wonders for your mood.（好好睡一覺對心情有奇效。）
  - 例：This new policy has done wonders for productivity.（這個新政策大幅提升了生產力。）
  - 例：Exercise does wonders for your health.（運動對健康有奇效。）
  - 常見搭配：do wonders for...（對⋯有奇效）、work wonders（產生驚人效果）
- **dialogue:** Your Hexgates have done wonders for our city.
  - Heimerdinger 對 Jayce 的肯定——你的海克斯之門為城市帶來了奇蹟般的改變。do wonders 的語氣是「超出預期地好」

## short notice
- **n.** a very brief amount of warning or advance time — 很短的預告時間、臨時通知
  - 例：Sorry for the short notice, but can we meet tomorrow?（抱歉這麼臨時，我們明天能見嗎？）
  - 例：They cancelled on short notice.（他們臨時取消了。）
  - 例：It's hard to find a replacement on such short notice.（這麼臨時要找替代人選很難。）
  - 常見搭配：on short notice（在很短的通知下）、at short notice（同義，較英式）、such short notice（這麼臨時）
- **dialogue:** It's short notice, I know.
  - Heimerdinger 自知時間很趕，但還是希望 Jayce 能端出東西來展示。語氣溫和帶歉意

## grand reveal
- **collocation** a dramatic, highly anticipated unveiling or presentation — 盛大的揭幕、壓軸展示
  - 例：The company is planning a grand reveal for their new product.（公司正在規劃新產品的盛大發表。）
  - 例：Save the best for the grand reveal.（把最好的留給壓軸揭幕。）
  - 補充：grand = 盛大的、隆重的；reveal = 揭示。合在一起是「吊足胃口後的正式公開」
- **dialogue:** People love a grand reveal.
  - Heimerdinger 很懂群眾心理——大家就是愛看壓軸大揭秘。這也在暗示 Jayce：你要給觀眾一個驚喜

## just the thing
- **idiom** exactly what is needed; the perfect solution — 正是需要的東西、剛好有
  - 例：This soup is just the thing for a cold day.（這碗湯正適合冷天喝。）
  - 例：I have just the thing to fix that.（我正好有東西可以修那個。）
  - 補充：語氣自信，帶有「剛剛好、不多不少」的感覺
- **dialogue:** Viktor and I have just the thing.
  - Jayce 自信回應——我跟 Viktor 正好有東西。語氣從剛才的謙虛瞬間切換到自信，顯示他對海克斯科技下一步的把握

## live up to
- **phrasal v.** to meet or fulfill expectations, standards, or a reputation — 達到、不辜負（期望、標準、名聲）
  - 例：The movie didn't live up to the hype.（這部電影沒有達到宣傳的期待。）
  - 例：She always lives up to her promises.（她總是說到做到。）
  - 例：It's hard to live up to your parents' expectations.（要達到父母的期望很難。）
  - 常見搭配：live up to expectations（不辜負期望）、live up to one's reputation（名副其實）、live up to the hype（達到炒作的期待）
- **dialogue:** You certainly have something to live up to, my boy.
  - Heimerdinger 最後這句暗藏深意。表面上是「你有很高的標準要追」，但結合剛才 Stanwick 的故事——真正的偉大不是個人成就，而是犧牲自我、成就他人。這是對 Jayce 的期許，也是整段對話的核心

## serves you right
- **idiom** used to say someone deserved the bad thing that happened to them — 活該、你自找的
  - 例：You didn't study and failed the exam? Serves you right.（你沒念書考砸了？活該。）
  - 例：He ate the whole cake and got a stomachache. Serves him right.（他把整個蛋糕吃完然後肚子痛。活該。）
  - 補充：語氣可輕可重。朋友間用帶調侃，認真場合用則有「你罪有應得」的意味
- **dialogue:** Serves you right.
  - Jayce 從後面偷彈 Caitlyn 的帽子想嚇她，結果被受過訓練的執法者反手壓制。Caitlyn 一句 "Serves you right"——誰叫你偷襲我。展現兩人從小認識的默契和打鬧關係

## pull strings
- **idiom** to use one's influence or connections secretly to get something done — 暗中動用關係、走後門
  - 例：He pulled some strings to get his son into the company.（他動用了一些關係讓兒子進公司。）
  - 例：I wonder who pulled strings to get her that promotion.（我很好奇是誰在背後幫她拿到升遷的。）
  - 補充：字面意思是「拉線」，源自操控傀儡的意象——在幕後操縱事情的走向
  - 常見搭配：pull strings for someone（為某人走後門）、pull some strings（動用一些關係）
- **dialogue:** I can only imagine the strings she pulled to have me posted here.
  - Caitlyn 無奈地說——我只能想像我媽動用了多少關係才把我安排到這個（安全無聊的）崗位。她想去前線辦案，卻被派到自家帳篷旁站崗

## front-row seats
- **collocation** the best possible position to observe something; a prime vantage point — 前排座位；（比喻）最佳觀賞位置
  - 例：We had front-row seats to the concert.（我們坐在演唱會的前排。）
  - 例：Living downtown gives you front-row seats to all the action.（住在市中心讓你能近距離看到所有大事。）
  - 補充：常用作比喻，不一定真的有「座位」，而是「你剛好在最佳位置能親眼目睹」
- **dialogue:** At least you'll have front-row seats to the speech.
  - Jayce 自嘲加安慰——你被派在這裡也不是全無好處，至少能第一排看我演講。語氣帶著得意和玩笑

## herd
- **v.** to drive or move animals in a group; figuratively, to try to manage or control a group of unruly people — 趕（牲口）；（比喻）管控一群不受控的人
  - 例：The dog herded the sheep into the pen.（牧羊犬把羊群趕進圈裡。）
  - 例：The teachers tried to herd the kids onto the bus.（老師們試著把孩子們趕上巴士。）
  - 常見搭配：herd cats（做不可能的事，因為貓不聽指揮）、herd people（趕人群）
- **dialogue:** Have fun herding the drunkards.
  - Jayce 的回擊——你不是在「工作」嗎？那就好好趕你的醉漢吧。把管理喝醉的群眾比喻成趕牲口，暗示這工作既無聊又荒謬

## be supposed to
- **sentence pattern** used to say what someone is expected or required to do, often implying they failed to do it — 應該要（做某事），常暗示沒做到
  - 例：You were supposed to call me yesterday.（你昨天應該要打給我的。）
  - 例：I'm supposed to be at the meeting in ten minutes.（我應該十分鐘內到會議室。）
  - 例：This wasn't supposed to happen.（這事不該發生的。）
  - 補充：用過去式 "were supposed to" 幾乎必定帶有指責語氣——「你本來該做但你沒做」。和 should 的差別在於 be supposed to 更強調外在的期望或安排
- **dialogue:** You were supposed to guard the cargo.
  - Sevika 對 Jinx 的怒斥——你被安排來看守貨物的，結果你跑去殺人，貨全被 Firelights 燒了。were supposed to 的語氣就是「你失職了」
- **dialogue:** Hextech was supposed to bring magic into the life of the common man. And now, it finally can.
  - 另一種用法——不是指責，而是修辭鋪陳。"was supposed to" 先點出「原本的理想（但一直做不到）」，再用 "And now, it finally can" 翻轉，製造激動人心的演講效果。這是簡報和演說中很常見的 setup-payoff 結構

## as much as
- **sentence pattern** used to compare the degree of something between two parties — 和⋯一樣（程度上）
  - 例：I love this city as much as you do.（我跟你一樣愛這座城市。）
  - 例：She cares about the project as much as anyone.（她跟任何人一樣在乎這個專案。）
  - 例：As much as I'd like to help, I can't.（儘管我很想幫忙，但我沒辦法。）
  - 補充：結尾的 do / does / did 是代替前面提過的動詞，避免重複。"as much as we do" = "as much as we believe science should be used to improve lives"
- **dialogue:** Heimerdinger believes science should be used to improve lives as much as we do.
  - Jayce 說 Heimerdinger 跟他們一樣相信科學應該改善生活。as much as we do 把自己和教授放在同一陣線，為接下來的展示做鋪墊

## like they asked
- **sentence pattern** a phrase appended to emphasize that something was done according to someone's request — 按照他們的要求。重複使用可強調「我們一直在聽命行事」
  - 例：We delivered the report like they asked.（我們按照他們的要求交了報告。）
  - 例：I finished it on time, like you asked.（我按照你的要求準時完成了。）
  - 補充：重複句式是一種修辭手法，用來累積情緒。Jayce 連用兩次 "like they asked" 暗示「我們夠了、該換我們做主了」
- **dialogue:** We stabilized the crystal like they asked. Built the Hexgates like they asked.
  - Jayce 對 Viktor 說——我們照議會要求穩定水晶、建海克斯之門，全做了。重複 like they asked 是在為下一句 "It's our turn" 做蓄力

## it's one's turn to
- **sentence pattern** used to say it's now someone's opportunity or right to do something — 輪到某人來⋯
  - 例：It's your turn to cook tonight.（今晚輪到你做飯。）
  - 例：We've listened long enough. It's our turn to speak.（我們聽夠了。輪到我們說了。）
  - 補充：搭配前面的鋪墊使用時，暗示「我們已經等了很久 / 付出了很多」
- **dialogue:** It's our turn to decide the future of Hextech.
  - 接在兩句 "like they asked" 之後，Jayce 表態——我們一直在配合，現在輪到我們決定方向了。語氣從順從轉為主導

## make it
- **phrasal v.** to manage to arrive at or attend something; to succeed — 趕到、出席；成功
  - 例：Sorry I'm late — I almost didn't make it.（抱歉遲到了——我差點趕不上。）
  - 例：I'm glad you could make it to the party.（很高興你能來派對。）
  - 例：She finally made it as a professional musician.（她終於成為了職業音樂家。）
  - 常見搭配：glad you could make it（很高興你能來）、make it on time（準時到達）、make it big（大獲成功）
- **dialogue:** Professor. I'm so glad you could make it.
  - Jayce 歡迎 Heimerdinger 到場。glad you could make it 是很常見的歡迎用語，帶有「我知道你很忙，感謝你撥空」的意思

## wouldn't miss it for the world
- **idiom** used to emphasize that you definitely want to attend or experience something — 絕不會錯過、就算給我全世界也不會錯過
  - 例：Your wedding? I wouldn't miss it for the world.（你的婚禮？我絕對不會錯過。）
  - 例：She said she wouldn't miss the concert for the world.（她說她無論如何都不會錯過這場演唱會。）
  - 補充：誇張的表達方式，語氣溫暖且真誠，表示對這件事非常重視
- **dialogue:** Wouldn't miss it for the world, my boy.
  - Heimerdinger 對 Jayce 說——絕不會錯過的。語氣慈祥，顯示他對 Jayce 的關愛和期待

## on the docket
- **idiom** scheduled or planned to be dealt with; on the agenda — 在議程上、待處理的事項
  - 例：What's on the docket for today's meeting?（今天的會議有什麼議程？）
  - 例：We have three items on the docket this afternoon.（今天下午有三個待處理事項。）
  - 補充：docket 原指法庭的案件排程表，引申為「接下來要處理/展示的東西」
- **dialogue:** What do we have on the docket?
  - Heimerdinger 問「今天要看什麼」，用 on the docket 帶有一種老派紳士的語氣

## the common man
- **collocation** ordinary people; people without special rank, status, or privilege — 一般人、平民百姓
  - 例：The policy was designed to benefit the common man.（這項政策是為了造福一般民眾。）
  - 例：He spoke in a language the common man could understand.（他用一般人能懂的語言說話。）
  - 補充：和 ordinary people 同義，但更正式、更有演說感。與精英或權貴階層相對
- **dialogue:** Hextech was supposed to bring magic into the life of the common man.
  - Jayce 強調海克斯科技的初衷——讓魔法走入平民的生活，不是被鎖在學院裡給少數人用

## a means to
- **collocation** a method or way of achieving something — 一種方法、一個手段
  - 例：Education is a means to a better life.（教育是通往更好生活的途徑。）
  - 例：We need to find a means to reduce costs.（我們需要找到降低成本的方法。）
  - 補充：means 在這裡是單數（a means），不是複數。a means to = a way to。常見搭配：a means to an end（達成目的的手段）、by all means（當然可以、盡管）
- **dialogue:** We've found a means to fortify the crystals.
  - Viktor 說「我們找到了強化水晶的方法」。用 a means to 比 a way to 更正式，符合向教授做報告的語境

## equipped with
- **collocation** fitted or provided with a particular feature or tool — 配備了⋯、裝備了⋯
  - 例：The car is equipped with advanced safety features.（這輛車配備了先進的安全功能。）
  - 例：All soldiers were equipped with body armor.（所有士兵都配備了防彈衣。）
  - 常見搭配：fully equipped（全副裝備的）、equipped with tools / weapons / skills（配備了工具／武器／技能）
- **dialogue:** A mechanical arm, equipped with a powerful ray of light.
  - Viktor 介紹 Hex Claw——一條機械手臂，配備了強力光束。equipped with 在產品展示中很常用

## kinks to iron out
- **idiom** minor problems or flaws that need to be resolved — 需要解決的小問題、待排除的小障礙
  - 例：The software works, but there are still a few kinks to iron out.（軟體能跑了，但還有一些小問題要解決。）
  - 例：We need to iron out the kinks before the launch.（我們需要在發佈前排除問題。）
  - 補充：kink 原指繩子的扭結，iron out = 熨平、消除。合在一起 = 把小毛病理順。語氣輕描淡寫，暗示問題不大
- **dialogue:** Obviously, there are a few kinks to iron out and screws to be tightened.
  - Heimerdinger 肯定了展示成果，但補充「還有些小問題要處理、螺絲要鎖緊」。screws to be tightened 是同義的機械比喻——還有細節要完善

## in the blink of an eye
- **idiom** in an extremely short time; almost instantly — 一眨眼之間、轉瞬之間
  - 例：The weekend flew by in the blink of an eye.（週末一眨眼就過了。）
  - 例：Everything can change in the blink of an eye.（一切都可能在一瞬間改變。）
  - 常見搭配：zip past in the blink of an eye（咻地一下就過了）
  - 補充：zip past = 咻地飛過，帶有速度感。zip 本身有「拉鏈快速拉過」的擬聲感
- **dialogue:** It zips past you in the blink of an eye.
  - Heimerdinger 安慰 Jayce——十年一眨眼就過了。但他活了幾百年，對人類壽命的 Jayce 和身體在惡化的 Viktor 來說，十年是奢侈品。這是整部劇核心衝突之一——不朽者與凡人對時間的認知落差

## with respect
- **sentence pattern** a polite phrase used before expressing disagreement — 恕我直言、冒昧說一句
  - 例：With respect, I think there's a better approach.（恕我直言，我覺得有更好的做法。）
  - 例：With all due respect, that's not what the data shows.（恕我冒昧，數據顯示的並非如此。）
  - 補充：表面禮貌，實際在表示「我接下來要說的話你可能不愛聽」。加了 all due 語氣更強
- **dialogue:** With respect, Professor.
  - Viktor 準備反駁 Heimerdinger 的「十年」提議。用 with respect 開頭，維持對長輩的禮貌但明確表達不同意

## keep at it
- **phrasal v.** to continue working hard at something; to persist — 堅持下去、繼續努力
  - 例：Learning a language is hard, but keep at it.（學語言很難，但堅持下去。）
  - 例：You're making progress — just keep at it.（你在進步——繼續努力就好。）
  - 補充：口語鼓勵用語，語氣正面但在某些語境下會顯得敷衍——「你繼續做吧（但我不會幫你加速）」
- **dialogue:** Keep at it and I'm sure you will discover a way to safeguard Hextech against misuse.
  - Heimerdinger 的語氣是鼓勵，但對 Viktor 來說聽起來像敷衍——「你慢慢來吧」。keep at it 在這裡恰好呼應了他們對時間的根本分歧

## safeguard against
- **phrasal v.** to protect or guard against a risk or danger — 防範、保護免受⋯
  - 例：We need to safeguard against data breaches.（我們需要防範資料外洩。）
  - 例：The law was designed to safeguard against abuse of power.（這條法律是為了防止濫權。）
  - 常見搭配：safeguard against misuse（防止濫用）、safeguard against threats（防範威脅）
- **dialogue:** Discover a way to safeguard Hextech against misuse.
  - Heimerdinger 的核心顧慮——力量給了所有人，就一定會有人濫用。他要求先解決安全問題。這也是他和 Jayce / Viktor 衝突的根源——保守的安全優先 vs. 急切的理想主義

## there must've been
- **sentence pattern** used to make a confident guess about a past situation — 一定有⋯、肯定是⋯。must have been 是對過去事實的高度確信推測
  - 例：There must've been hundreds of people at the concert.（演唱會上一定有好幾百人。）
  - 例：He must've been exhausted after the trip.（旅行之後他一定累壞了。）
  - 補充：must have + p.p. 是對過去的推測，語氣比 probably 更確定。口語中 must have 常縮寫成 must've
- **dialogue:** There must've been four, five of them. I don't know how they got inside.
  - 收過賄的港口職員在被調查時裝無辜——「大概有四五個人吧，我不知道他們怎麼進來的」。用 must've been 假裝自己也只是在猜測

## why does that not surprise me
- **sentence pattern** a rhetorical question expressing that something was entirely expected — 為什麼我一點都不意外。修辭性反問，語氣帶有厭煩和「又來了」的感覺
  - 例：He's late again? Why does that not surprise me.（他又遲到了？為什麼我一點都不意外。）
  - 例：She forgot the deadline — why does that not surprise me.（她忘了截止日——我怎麼一點都不驚訝。）
  - 補充：不是真的在問「為什麼」，而是表達「我早就知道會這樣」。語氣從疲憊到嘲諷都有
- **dialogue:** Caitlyn Kiramman, why does that not surprise me?
  - Marcus 一看到 Caitlyn 就用全名叫她，配上這句反問——意思是「又是你，我早就料到了」

## interfere in
- **phrasal v.** to involve oneself in something without being asked or wanted — 干預、插手。帶有「你不該管」的負面意味
  - 例：Stop interfering in my personal life.（別再干涉我的私生活了。）
  - 例：The government was accused of interfering in the election.（政府被指控干預選舉。）
  - 常見搭配：interfere in someone's affairs（干涉別人的事）、interfere with（妨礙、干擾——注意介系詞不同，意思也不同）
- **dialogue:** Interfering in an investigation, again.
  - Marcus 指責 Caitlyn——又在干預調查了。again 放在句尾加重「不是第一次了」的語氣

## take priority
- **collocation** to be more important or urgent than other things — 優先、更重要
  - 例：Safety always takes priority over speed.（安全永遠優先於速度。）
  - 例：Family takes priority for me.（家庭對我來說是第一位的。）
  - 常見搭配：take priority over（比⋯更優先）、top priority（最高優先）、high priority（高優先級）
- **dialogue:** And clearly, this takes priority.
  - Caitlyn 的反擊——看守帳篷很重要，但眼前這個案子明顯更緊急。用 clearly 加強「這不是顯而易見的嗎」的語氣

## be used to getting one's way
- **idiom** to be accustomed to always having things go the way one wants — 習慣凡事如願、習慣想要什麼就得到什麼
  - 例：He's used to getting his way at work.（他在公司習慣了說了算。）
  - 例：She's so used to getting her way that she can't handle rejection.（她太習慣事事順心，完全受不了被拒絕。）
  - 補充：get one's way = 如願以償、達到目的。be used to getting one's way 暗指對方被慣壞了、不懂規矩
- **dialogue:** I realize you're used to getting your way, Kiramman.
  - Marcus 暗指 Caitlyn 是大小姐——在家呼風喚雨慣了，但在這裡不行。故意叫姓氏不叫名字，用距離感強調上下級關係

## chain of command
- **n.** the hierarchical structure of authority in an organization — 指揮鏈、命令體系
  - 例：You need to follow the chain of command.（你得遵守指揮鏈。）
  - 例：He bypassed the chain of command and went straight to the CEO.（他跳過指揮鏈直接找了 CEO。）
  - 常見搭配：follow the chain of command（遵守指揮鏈）、break the chain of command（打破指揮鏈）
  - 補充：源自軍事用語，現廣泛用於公司、警隊等有層級的組織
- **dialogue:** We have a chain of command for a reason.
  - Marcus 用官階壓 Caitlyn——我們有上下級制度是有道理的，你一個基層不該越級行動。for a reason 放在句尾 = 「這不是沒道理的」

## there's more going on than
- **sentence pattern** used to say a situation is more complex than it appears on the surface — 事情不只是表面看到的那樣、背後還有更多
  - 例：There's more going on here than meets the eye.（這裡的事情比看到的更複雜。）
  - 例：I think there's more going on than he's telling us.（我覺得他沒有全部說出來。）
  - 補充：很實用的句型——當你覺得某件事背後有隱情時可以用
- **dialogue:** There's more going on here than just the smuggling.
  - Caitlyn 試圖說服 Marcus——這不只是普通走私。但 Marcus 根本不想讓她查下去，因為他自己就牽涉其中

## I'll take it from here
- **idiom** I will handle the rest; you can stop now — 剩下的交給我、從這裡開始我來接手
  - 例：Thanks for your help. I'll take it from here.（謝謝你的幫忙。剩下的我來。）
  - 例：You've done enough — I'll take it from here.（你做得夠多了——接下來我處理。）
  - 補充：語氣可以是感謝（謝謝你，我接手），也可以是命令（你可以走了）。這裡 Marcus 是後者——直接打斷 Caitlyn 不讓她繼續
- **dialogue:** I'll take it from here.
  - Marcus 打斷 Caitlyn 的問話，接管案件。表面是上司接手，實際是阻止她挖到真相

## graveyard shift
- **n.** the work shift that covers the late night to early morning hours, typically midnight to 8 a.m. — 大夜班、凌晨班
  - 例：She's been working the graveyard shift for three months.（她已經上了三個月的大夜班。）
  - 例：Nobody wants the graveyard shift.（沒人想上大夜班。）
  - 補充：graveyard = 墓地，因為深夜時段安靜得像墓地。三個班次：day shift（日班）、swing shift（小夜班）、graveyard shift（大夜班）
- **dialogue:** You can take the graveyard shift tonight at the fair.
  - Marcus 的懲罰——你不是閒不住嗎？那就去市集上值最爛的班。用 "since you're looking for more work" 的語氣嘲諷她多管閒事

## fire on
- **phrasal v.** to shoot at; to direct gunfire toward — 向⋯開火
  - 例：The soldiers fired on the approaching vehicles.（士兵向駛來的車輛開火。）
  - 例：The police were forced to fire on the suspects.（警方被迫向嫌犯開火。）
  - 常見搭配：fire on the enemy（向敵人開火）、fire at（朝⋯射擊）
  - 補充：fire on 和 fire at 都能用，on 更強調主動攻擊性
- **dialogue:** She fired on us.
  - Sevika 向 Silco 告狀——Jinx 朝我們自己人開火了。一句話點出 Jinx 的失控程度

## freeze up
- **phrasal v.** to suddenly become unable to move, speak, or act due to fear or panic — 僵住、當機
  - 例：I froze up during the presentation and forgot everything.（我簡報時僵住了，什麼都忘了。）
  - 例：She froze up when she saw the spider.（她看到蜘蛛就僵住了。）
  - 補充：freeze 的過去式是 froze。freeze up 強調因為心理因素（恐懼、緊張、創傷）而無法行動
- **dialogue:** She froze up and lost her shit.

## lose one's shit
- **informal** to completely lose control of one's emotions; to freak out — 完全失控、抓狂、崩潰
  - 例：He lost his shit when he found out he got fired.（他發現自己被開除時徹底抓狂了。）
  - 例：Don't lose your shit over something so small.（別為了這麼小的事崩潰。）
  - 補充：非常粗俗的口語，比 lose control 或 freak out 強烈得多。Sevika 刻意選最粗的說法來表達她的憤怒和不屑
- **dialogue:** She froze up and lost her shit.

## could've handled
- **sentence pattern** could have + past participle — used to say someone had the ability to do something but didn't get to — 本來可以處理的（但沒能）
  - 例：I could've handled it myself if you'd let me.（如果你讓我來，我自己就能處理。）
  - 例：We could've handled the situation differently.（我們本來可以用不同方式處理這件事。）
  - 補充：could have + p.p. = 過去有能力做但最終沒做成。語氣帶有遺憾或不滿——「我明明可以，但被搞砸了」
- **dialogue:** I could've handled those brats.
  - Sevika 的不滿——那些 Firelights 我自己就能解決，根本不需要 Jinx 來搗亂

## expect better from someone
- **collocation** to believe someone should have done more or performed at a higher standard — 對某人有更高的期望、期望某人做得更好
  - 例：I expected better from you.（我以為你會做得更好。）
  - 例：The coach expects better from his players.（教練對球員有更高的期望。）
  - 補充：通常用在失望的語境——「你讓我失望了」。比直接說 "I'm disappointed" 更有壓迫感，因為暗示「你本來配得上更高的標準」
- **dialogue:** I expect better from you than excuses.

## make sure things go smoothly

## cut off

## leave someone behind

## as well as I do

  - 補充：常用來說「這事不用我多講，你自己心裡有數」。語氣可以是理性的陳述，也可以帶有「別裝了」的意味

## screw-up
