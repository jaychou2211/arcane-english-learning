# Episode 01

## above it all
- **idiom** detached from everyday problems or troubles
  - 例：She always seems above it all.（她總是看起來超然世外。）
  - 常見搭配：rise above it all（超越這一切）、get above it all（脫離這一切）
- **dialogue:** It's nice getting above it all, huh?
  - Vi 帶 Powder 爬上屋頂，字面上在一切之上，同時也暫時脫離了底下的苦日子

## couldn't we have...?
- **sentence pattern** used to complain or express regret about a past choice
  - 例：Couldn't we have taken a taxi?（我們難道不能搭計程車嗎？）
  - 例：Couldn't we have started earlier?（我們難道不能早點開始嗎？）
- **dialogue:** Couldn't we have at least just walked there?
  - 抱怨 Vi 帶大家走屋頂，明明可以用走的

## out of sight
- **idiom** hidden; not able to be seen
  - 例：Keep the presents out of sight.（把禮物藏好別被看到。）
- **dialogue:** Gotta stay out of sight for this one.
  - Vi 解釋為什麼要走屋頂——這趟不能被人看見

## called it
- **informal** said when you correctly predicted something
  - 例：I knew it would rain. Called it.（我就知道會下雨，果然吧。）
- **dialogue:** Called it.
  - Mylo 早就料到 Powder 不敢跳，得意地說「我就說吧」

## on you
- **informal** your fault; your responsibility
  - 例：If this goes wrong, it's on you.（要是出事，那是你的錯。）
- **dialogue:** This is on you, Vi.
  - Mylo 怪 Vi 帶 Powder 來，害大家被拖累

## all the way
- **adv.** the entire distance; emphasizes how far or how much effort
  - 例：We walked all the way home.（我們一路走回家。）
  - 例：Did you carry this all the way up here?（你把這個搬上來的？大老遠欸。）
- **dialogue:** all the way up here
  - Claggor抱怨費了好大勁才爬到屋頂上來

## any time soon
- **adv.** in the near future; used almost exclusively in negatives or rhetorical questions to imply something is NOT happening soon enough
  - 例：This isn't going to end any time soon.（短期內不會結束。）
  - 例：I don't see that happening any time soon.（我看短期內不會發生。）
  - 常見搭配：
    - 否定句 + any time soon — 短期內不會
    - 疑問句 + any time soon? — 不耐煩地催促
- **dialogue:** You gonna get that door open any time soon?
  - Vi 用反問句催 Mylo 開鎖快一點

## seeing as
- **conjunction** (informal) because; since; given that — used to introduce a reason
  - 例：Seeing as you're not busy, can you help me?（既然你沒事，能幫個忙嗎？）
  - 例：Seeing as it's raining, let's stay home.（既然在下雨，我們待在家吧。）
- **dialogue:** Seeing as I'm the only one who knows how to pick locks, I suggest--
  - Mylo 用「既然就只有我會開鎖」當理由，帶著嗆 Vi 別催他的語氣

## for once
- **adv.** for the first time; unusually — implies the person normally doesn't do this
  - 例：For once, I agree with you.（難得我同意你一次。）
  - 例：For once, he was on time.（他破天荒地準時了。）
- **dialogue:** You know, Claggor, for once you're right.
  - Mylo 損 Claggor「你難得說對了」，承認他們確實不該在這裡

## keep an eye out
- **idiom** to watch for something; to stay alert and look for it
  - 例：Keep an eye out for any spelling errors.（留意有沒有拼字錯誤。）
  - 例：Keep an eye out for a gas station.（注意看有沒有加油站。）
- **dialogue:** Keep an eye out for anything that looks valuable, Powder.
  - Vi 叫 Powder 留意值錢的東西

## Simple, just...
- **sentence pattern** a sarcastic reply that pretends to offer a simple solution, but the "solution" is actually impossible or out of one's control
  - 例："How do I get rich?" — "Simple, just have rich parents."
  - 例："How do I become taller?" — "Simple, just pick different genes."
  - 補充：get born = be born 的口語說法
- **dialogue:** Simple, just get born lucky.
  - Claggor 問怎麼有人能有這麼多東西，Mylo 諷刺地說「投胎投對就好了」

## fill a ___ with
- **sentence pattern** an exaggeration to say there's way too much of something
  - 例：You could fill a book with his excuses.（他的藉口多到可以出一本書。）
  - 例：You could fill a room with all the junk he collects.（他收的破爛多到可以塞滿一間房。）
- **dialogue:** You could fill a damn library with all the things you didn't do.
  - Mylo 抓住 Powder 說的 "I didn't do anything" 反過來諷刺她——你「沒做的事」多到能塞滿一座圖書館

## right under someone's nose
- **idiom** right in front of someone without them noticing
  - 例：He stole it right under my nose.（他在我眼皮底下偷走的。）
  - 例：The answer was right under our noses the whole time.（答案一直就在我們眼前。）
- **dialogue:** We just emptied a Piltover penthouse right under the enforcers' noses.
  - 在執法官眼皮底下幹完，他們卻沒發現

## beat yourself up
- **phrasal v.** to blame or criticize yourself too harshly
  - 例：Don't beat yourself up over it.（別為這件事太自責了。）
  - 例：He keeps beating himself up about the mistake.（他一直在為那個錯誤自責。）
- **dialogue:** So, if you're done beating yourselves up, let's get this home.
  - Vi 叫大家別再互相指責了，趕快把東西帶回家

## You could say that.
- **sentence pattern** a vague, noncommittal reply that neither confirms nor denies; an understatement implying "yes, but I'm not going to elaborate"
  - 例："Was the exam hard?" — "You could say that."（「考試難嗎？」——「算是吧。」）
  - 例："Are you a fan of coffee?" — "You could say that. I drink five cups a day."（「你喜歡咖啡？」——「可以這麼說吧，我一天喝五杯。」）
- **dialogue:** You could say that.
  - Mylo 被問到是否撈到好東西，用輕描淡寫的方式帶過，不想透露太多細節

## kick the nest
- **idiom** short for "kick the hornet's nest"; to stir up trouble by provoking someone or something dangerous
  - 例：You really kicked the nest with that comment.（你那句話真的捅了馬蜂窩。）
  - 例：Don't kick the hornet's nest — just leave it alone.（別去惹事——別管它就好。）
  - 常見搭配：kick the hornet's nest（捅馬蜂窩）、stir the nest（攪亂局面）
- **dialogue:** Someone really kicked the nest, huh?
  - Deckard 假裝閒聊，暗示有人在上城惹了大麻煩，試探 Vi 他們是不是就是那群人

## trouble finds you
- **idiom** trouble will come to you whether you want it or not — used as a veiled threat or a fatalistic warning
  - 例：You can try to avoid conflict, but sometimes trouble finds you.（你可以試著避開衝突，但有時候麻煩會自己找上門。）
  - 例：I wasn't looking for a fight, but trouble found me.（我不是想打架，但麻煩自己找上來了。）
  - 補充：常用來反駁 "I don't want any trouble"，把對方的退讓堵回去
- **dialogue:** You know, in my experience, trouble finds you.
  - Claggor 說「我們不想惹事」，Deckard 用這句話威脅回去——你不找麻煩？沒關係，麻煩會來找你，而我就是那個麻煩

## get ugly
- **idiom** (of a situation) to become violent, hostile, or unpleasant
  - 例：Things got ugly after the argument.（吵完之後事情就變得很難看了。）
  - 例：If you don't pay up, this is going to get ugly.（你不付錢的話，事情就不好看了。）
  - 常見搭配：things get ugly（事情變難看）、no reason to get ugly（沒必要搞難看）
- **dialogue:** There's no reason this has to get ugly.
  - Deckard 表面上好言相勸，實際上是在暗示：不配合的話就要動粗了

## a taste of
- **idiom** a small sample or portion of something; a brief experience of something
  - 例：Here, have a taste of this cake.（來，嚐一口這個蛋糕。）
  - 例：He got a taste of what it's like to be famous.（他體驗了一下當名人的感覺。）
  - 例：Just give me a taste of the profits.（分我一小份利潤就好。）
- **dialogue:** How about you share a little taste of your treasure there
  - Deckard 故意說得像只要「一點點」，降低對方的抗拒感，實際上就是在勒索

## call it even
- **idiom** to agree that neither side owes the other anything; to consider things settled and fair
  - 例：You bought lunch last time, I'll get it today — let's call it even.（上次你請午餐，今天我請——這樣扯平。）
  - 例：Just give me $20 and we'll call it even.（給我 20 塊我們就算了。）
- **dialogue:** and we'll call it even?
  - Deckard 把勒索包裝成公平交易——你們分點東西出來，我們就兩不相欠了

## Wanna see how that ends?
- **sentence pattern** a rhetorical counter-threat; daring someone to follow through on their threat, implying it won't end well for them
  - 例："I'll report you to the manager." — "Wanna see how that ends?"（「我要跟經理告狀。」——「你要試試看結果會怎樣嗎？」）
  - 例：He pulled out a knife. She stepped closer and said, "Wanna see how that ends?"（他掏出刀，她走近一步說：「你想看看結局會是什麼嗎？」）
- **dialogue:** Wanna see how that ends?
  - Deckard 被打得狼狽還掏刀，Vi 完全不怕，走上前用反問直接把威脅頂回去

## fight off
- **phrasal v.** to resist and drive away an attacker or threat
  - 例：She fought off the attacker with her bag.（她用包包擊退了攻擊者。）
  - 例：He managed to fight off three guys at once.（他竟然同時擋住了三個人。）
  - 例：I'm trying to fight off a cold.（我在努力抵抗感冒。）
- **dialogue:** I tried to fight him off with Mouser.
  - Powder 解釋她試著用自己做的小裝置 Mouser 把攻擊者打退，不是故意丟掉東西的

## Who saw that coming?
- **sentence pattern** a sarcastic rhetorical question meaning "that was totally predictable"; the speaker pretends to be surprised, but actually means the opposite
  - 例："The cheapest option broke on day one." — "Who saw that coming?"（「最便宜的那個第一天就壞了。」——「誰猜得到呢？」）
  - 例：He didn't study and failed the exam. Who saw that coming?（他沒讀書然後考砸了，真是出乎意料呢。）
  - 補充：語氣等同於 "I told you so" 或 "obviously"
- **dialogue:** Who saw that coming?
  - Powder 說 Mouser 沒用，Mylo 馬上諷刺——他本來就覺得 Powder 的東西靠不住，結果完全在預料之中

## never should have
- **sentence pattern** used to express strong regret about a past action; implies the decision was a mistake from the start
  - 例：I never should have trusted him.（我根本就不該相信他。）
  - 例：We never should have sold that house.（我們根本就不該賣那棟房子。）
  - 補充：比 "shouldn't have" 語氣更重，"never" 強調這個決定從頭到尾都是錯的
- **dialogue:** We never should have gone over there.
  - Claggor 沮喪地感嘆，整趟去上城區的行動根本就不值得

## get one's face bashed in
- **idiom** to get severely beaten in the face
  - 例：He got his face bashed in during the fight.（他在打架時被揍得滿臉開花。）
  - 例：Keep talking like that and you'll get your face bashed in.（繼續這樣講話，你會被打到臉腫。）
  - 常見搭配：get bashed in（被猛砸）、bash someone's face in（把某人的臉打爛）
- **dialogue:** I get my face bashed in, and she just gets a pass?
  - Mylo 不滿自己跟 Deckard 那群人打架挨了揍，結果闖禍的 Powder 卻被 Vi 直接原諒

## get a pass
- **idiom** to be excused or let off without punishment
  - 例：He broke the rules but got a pass because it was his first time.（他犯了規但因為是第一次所以被放過了。）
  - 例：Why does she always get a pass?（為什麼她每次都沒事？）
- **dialogue:** I get my face bashed in, and she just gets a pass?
  - Mylo 覺得不公平——東西弄丟是 Powder 的錯，他還因此被打，結果 Powder 什麼責都不用負

## drop it
- **phrasal v.** to stop talking about something; to let a subject go
  - 例：I don't want to argue. Just drop it.（我不想吵，別說了。）
  - 例：She kept asking, but he told her to drop it.（她一直問，但他叫她別再提了。）
- **dialogue:** Just drop it, Mylo.
  - Vi 叫 Mylo 閉嘴別再怪 Powder，話題到此為止

## conclude our business
- **idiom** a formal way to say a deal or transaction is finished
  - 例：If there's nothing else, I think that concludes our business.（如果沒別的事，我們的交易就到此為止了。）
  - 例：We shook hands and concluded our business.（我們握了手，把生意談完了。）
- **dialogue:** I suppose that concludes our business, then.
  - 在 Vander 的店裡，兩個看起來不好惹的傢伙跟一個戴眼鏡的人做完交易，客氣地收場

## wares
- **n.** goods or merchandise for sale, often used in trade or market contexts
  - 例：The merchant displayed his wares at the market.（商人在市場上展示他的貨物。）
  - 例：Nobody's buying your wares at that price.（那個價格沒人會買你的貨。）
- **dialogue:** Demand for your wares has dropped since we made our arrangements.
  - 不好惹的傢伙用「你的貨行情跌了」當藉口，片面壓價坑眼鏡仔

## arrangements
- **n.** terms or conditions that were agreed upon in advance; a deal
  - 例：We had an arrangement — you weren't supposed to tell anyone.（我們說好了——你不該告訴任何人。）
  - 例：Let's stick to our original arrangement.（照我們原本講好的來。）
- **dialogue:** Demand for your wares has dropped since we made our arrangements.
  - "our arrangements" 指雙方先前談好的交易條件，現在被單方面推翻

## shake on it
- **idiom** to seal a deal or agreement with a handshake; implies a binding promise
  - 例：We shook on it, so you can't back out now.（我們握手說好了，你不能反悔。）
  - 例：Let's shake on it.（來握手成交吧。）
- **dialogue:** We shook on it.
  - 眼鏡仔發現對方片面壓價，急忙搬出「我們可是握手說好的」來抗議，但對方顯然不在乎什麼握手承諾，就是要吃定他

## Such are the risks of...
- **sentence pattern** a formal, dismissive way to say "that's just how it goes"; used to brush off someone's complaint by framing the loss as an unavoidable part of the game
  - 例：Such are the risks of investing.（投資就是這樣，有賺有賠。）
  - 例：Such are the risks of trusting strangers.（相信陌生人就是會有這種風險。）
- **dialogue:** Such are the risks of business.
  - 眼鏡仔抗議被壓價，對方冷冷回一句「做生意就是這樣」，用一句看似講道理的話把他的抗議全部擋回去，實際上就是「你能怎樣？」

## You don't seem to listen
- **sentence pattern** a condescending way to tell someone they're not getting the message; implies the other person is either stupid or deliberately ignoring you
  - 例：You don't seem to listen — I said no.（你好像聽不懂人話——我說了不行。）
  - 例：You don't seem to listen, so let me say it one more time.（你好像沒在聽，那我再說一次。）
- **dialogue:** You don't seem to listen, barman--
  - Vander 插話想調停，對方用「你好像聽不懂」加上輕蔑的稱呼 "barman"（酒保）來貶低他，意思是：這不關你的事，一個調酒的少插嘴。結果話還沒說完就被 Vander 教做人了

## Don't threaten the guy who...
- **sentence pattern** a witty comeback that reframes the speaker's role as someone you don't want to mess with; the humor comes from using a mundane description to deliver a veiled threat
  - 例：Don't threaten the guy who cooks your food.（別威脅那個幫你做飯的人。）
  - 例：Don't threaten the guy who knows your passwords.（別威脅那個知道你所有密碼的人。）
- **dialogue:** Don't threaten the guy who pours the drinks.
  - 對方剛用 "barman" 貶低 Vander，Vander 直接把這個身分接過來反將一軍——你叫我酒保？那你最好別惹幫你倒酒的人。表面上幽默，實際上是在說：你在我的地盤，別不識相

## I expected something + adj.
- **sentence pattern** a dismissive remark upon meeting someone or seeing something for the first time; implies disappointment or underwhelment — using "something" instead of "someone" adds extra condescension by treating the person like an object
  - 例：I expected something bigger.（我以為會大一點。）
  - 例：I expected something more impressive.（我以為會更厲害一點。）
- **dialogue:** I expected something… younger.
  - 女性角色見到大名鼎鼎的「底城獵犬」Vander 本人後，故意停頓再說 "younger"——久仰大名，但見到本人嘛⋯⋯有點老了吧。用 "something" 而不是 "someone"，把人當東西評價，更添輕蔑

## honor one's word
- **idiom** to keep a promise; to do what you said you would do
  - 例：He always honors his word.（他一向說到做到。）
  - 例：If you can't honor your word, don't make promises.（做不到就別承諾。）
  - 常見搭配：a man/woman of his/her word（說話算話的人）、keep one's word（守信）、go back on one's word（食言）
- **dialogue:** We were expecting traders who would honor their word, so I guess we're all disappointed.
  - 對方用 "I expected something younger" 嘲諷 Vander，Vander 用同樣的 "expecting" 結構反擊——你嫌我老？我還嫌你們不守信呢。最後一句 "we're all disappointed" 把雙方拉平：彼此彼此，誰也別嫌誰

## get someone all wrong
- **idiom** to completely misunderstand someone's character or intentions
  - 例：You got me all wrong — I was trying to help.（你完全誤會我了——我是想幫忙。）
  - 例：People get him all wrong; he's actually really kind.（大家都誤解他了，他其實人很好。）
- **dialogue:** You got us all wrong, my friend. We were just… negotiating.
  - 被 Vander 當場揭穿欺壓眼鏡仔之後，惡霸馬上改口裝無辜——「你誤會了朋友，我們只是在⋯⋯談價錢」。"my friend" 聽起來親切，實際上是在示弱求和；刻意停頓再說 "negotiating"，硬把明擺著的勒索說成正常交易

## speak someone's language
- **idiom** to say something that the listener agrees with or wants to hear; to communicate in a way that resonates
  - 例：Free pizza? Now you're speaking my language.（免費披薩？這我就懂了。）
  - 例：When she mentioned a raise, she was really speaking my language.（她提到加薪的時候，我就來勁了。）
- **dialogue:** Now you're speaking my language.
  - 對方改口說「我們只是在談生意」，Vander 馬上順著接——「這就對了嘛」。表面上是接受對方的說法，實際上是在說：你乖乖配合就好，早這樣不就沒事了？

## owe
- **v.** to be required to pay or give something to someone because of a debt or obligation
  - 例：You owe me $50.（你欠我 50 塊。）
  - 例：I owe you an apology.（我欠你一個道歉。）
  - 常見搭配：owe someone money（欠某人錢）、owe someone a favor（欠某人一個人情）、owe someone an explanation（欠某人一個解釋）
- **dialogue:** How about you just give Huck the rest of what you owe him.
  - Vander 直接點出：把欠 Huck（眼鏡仔）的餘款付清

## walk out of here
- **idiom** to leave a place on your own feet; often used in threats to imply the alternative is being carried out (injured or dead)
  - 例：Cooperate, and you walk out of here.（配合的話，你可以自己走出去。）
  - 例：Nobody walks out of here until I get answers.（在我得到答案之前，誰也別想走。）

## in one piece
- **idiom** unharmed; without injury; intact
  - 例：We made it home in one piece.（我們平安到家了。）
  - 例：Just get me there in one piece.（把我平安送到就好。）
- **dialogue:** and I'll let you walk out of here in one piece?
  - Vander 用最客氣的語氣講最狠的話——「付清欠款，我就讓你們完完整整地走出去」。"walk out of here" 暗示不配合就走不出去，"in one piece" 暗示不配合就不會是完整的。包裝成提議，實際上是最後通牒

## You'll learn to love it.
- **sentence pattern** a confident, slightly teasing way to tell someone they'll eventually come around to liking something they currently dislike; "learn to" here means "gradually come to", not literally "study"
  - 例：The coffee here is strong, but you'll learn to love it.（這裡的咖啡很烈，但你會慢慢愛上的。）
  - 例：I hated running at first, but I learned to love it.（我一開始討厭跑步，但後來愛上了。）
- **dialogue:** You'll learn to love it.
  - 對方剛嫌煙斗 "vile"，Vander 完全不在意，輕鬆回一句「你會愛上的」——潛台詞是：你以後會常來，跟我打交道的日子還長呢。既自信又帶點調情的味道

## learns none of this
- **sentence pattern** a warning that someone must not find out about what happened; "none of this" refers to everything that just took place
  - 例：Mom learns none of this, got it?（這些事不能讓媽知道，懂嗎？）
  - 例：The boss learns none of this.（這事不能傳到老闆耳裡。）
- **dialogue:** Vander learns none of this.
