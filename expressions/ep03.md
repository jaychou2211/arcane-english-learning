# Episode 03

## Ever wonder...?
- **sentence pattern** a casual way to open a thought-provoking question; short for "Have you ever wondered...?" — drops the subject and auxiliary verb for a conversational, almost rhetorical tone
  - 例：Ever wonder why the sky is blue?（你有沒有想過天空為什麼是藍的？）
  - 例：Ever wonder what would happen if you just quit?（你有沒有想過如果你直接辭職會怎樣？）
  - 補充：完整句是 "Have you ever wondered..."，省略後語氣更隨性、更像在自言自語
- **dialogue:** Ever wonder what it's like to drown?
  - Silco 的開場白。用最輕鬆的語氣問最沉重的問題——你想過溺水是什麼感覺嗎？像在聊天，但聊的是生死

## let it in
- **phrasal v.** to allow something to enter; to stop resisting and accept something — 讓它進來、放手接受
  - 例：Open the window and let the air in.（打開窗戶讓空氣進來。）
  - 例：She finally let the sadness in.（她終於讓悲傷進入心裡。）
  - 例：Don't let doubt in.（別讓懷疑鑽進來。）
- **dialogue:** Whisperin' in low tones to let it in.
  - 水在低語叫你「讓它進來」——字面上是讓水灌進身體，深層意思是放棄抵抗、接受死亡

## fade away
- **phrasal v.** to gradually disappear or become weaker until gone — 逐漸消失、慢慢淡去
  - 例：The sound faded away into silence.（聲音漸漸消失在寂靜中。）
  - 例：His anger slowly faded away.（他的怒氣慢慢消退了。）
  - 例：Old traditions are fading away.（舊傳統正在慢慢消失。）
  - 常見搭配：fade away into nothing（消失得無影無蹤）、slowly fade away（慢慢淡去）
- **dialogue:** And every problem in the world will fade away.
  - 水的承諾——只要你放手，所有問題都會消失。聽起來像解脫，其實是死亡的誘惑

## all the while
- **adv.** during the entire time; throughout the whole process — 在這整段時間裡、自始至終
  - 例：He was smiling, but all the while he was planning to leave.（他一直在笑，但其實整個過程中他都在計劃離開。）
  - 例：She pretended to listen, all the while thinking about something else.（她假裝在聽，但其實整個時候都在想別的事。）
  - 補充：比 "the whole time" 更文學、更有敘事感
- **dialogue:** And all the while, this question lingers before you.
  - 在你一邊感受平靜、一邊瘋狂掙扎的整個過程中，始終有一個問題懸在你面前

## have had enough
- **idiom** to have reached the limit of what one can tolerate; to be unable to take any more — 受夠了、到極限了
  - 例：I've had enough of this nonsense.（我受夠這些胡鬧了。）
  - 例：She's had enough and decided to quit.（她受夠了，決定辭職。）
  - 例：Have you had enough, or do you want more?（你夠了沒，還是還要？）
  - 補充：語氣可以從疲憊到憤怒，取決於情境
- **dialogue:** Have you had enough?
  - 溺水時的終極問題——你被折磨夠了嗎？要放棄還是繼續撐下去？Silco 的答案是繼續撐

## pass a lifetime
- **sentence pattern** to spend or go through an entire life; "pass" here means to spend (time), not to die — 度過一生
  - 例：You could pass a lifetime searching for the answer.（你可以花一輩子去找答案。）
  - 例：Some people pass a lifetime without knowing real love.（有些人一輩子都不知道真正的愛是什麼。）
  - 補充：pass time = 打發時間、度過時間，和 pass away（過世）不同
- **dialogue:** You could pass a lifetime without ever facing a choice like that.
  - 大多數人一輩子都不會被逼到「要不要放棄活下去」這種選擇面前。Silco 被逼到了——而那個經歷永遠改變了他

## I'm proud of you.
- **sentence pattern** a direct expression of pride in someone; used to affirm someone's character, choices, or achievements — 我以你為傲
  - 例：Your mom would be so proud of you.（你媽媽會以你為傲的。）
  - 例：I'm proud of you for standing up for yourself.（你為自己挺身而出，我很驕傲。）
  - 例：We're all proud of you.（我們都以你為傲。）
  - 補充：proud of + 人（以某人為傲）、proud of + 事（為某事感到驕傲）。對晚輩或親近的人說這句話份量非常重
- **dialogue:** I'm proud of you.
  - Vander 找到 Vi 後第一句不是責備、不是問為什麼，而是直接說「我以你為傲」。Vi 主動聯繫 Grayson 要犧牲自己保護大家——Vander 看到的不是她闖的禍，而是她願意扛責任的那顆心

## Always have been.
- **sentence pattern** short for "I always have been (proud of you)"; uses present perfect to emphasize that the feeling has been continuous from the past up to now — 一直都是
  - 例：You're my best friend. Always have been.（你是我最好的朋友。一直都是。）
  - 例：She's been strong. Always has been.（她一直都很堅強。一直都是。）
  - 補充：省略主詞和重複的部分，只留 "Always have been"，語氣簡短但份量極重——不是「從現在開始」，而是「從頭到尾、從來沒變過」
- **dialogue:** Always have been.
  - 接在 "I'm proud of you" 後面，Vander 補上這句等於在說：不是因為你今天做了什麼我才驕傲，是從以前到現在一直都是。這是一個知道自己即將赴死的父親，把最重要的話留給女兒

## make the arrest
- **collocation** the formal way to say "carry out an arrest"; used by law enforcement — 執行逮捕
  - 例：Officers moved in to make the arrest.（警員上前執行逮捕。）
  - 例：Who's going to make the arrest?（誰去執行逮捕？）
  - 補充：比 "arrest someone" 更正式、更像公務用語，強調「執行這個動作」而非針對某個人
- **dialogue:** You gonna let us make the arrest or not?
  - Marcus 催促 Grayson 動手——人都來了你還在拖什麼？語氣急躁，和 Grayson、Vander 之間的默契形成對比

## put someone away
- **phrasal v.** (informal) to send someone to prison — 把某人關進監獄
  - 例：They put him away for ten years.（他們把他關了十年。）
  - 例：He deserves to be put away.（他活該被關。）
  - 補充：口語用法，比 imprison 輕鬆但意思一樣。也可以指「把東西收起來」——put your toys away（把玩具收好）
- **dialogue:** I'm not putting you away, Vander.
  - Grayson 用口語而非官腔對 Vander 說話——我不是要關你。語氣帶著無奈和私交，她不想這樣做但身不由己

## pound of flesh
- **idiom** a debt or penalty that is legally owed but morally cruel to demand; from Shakespeare's *The Merchant of Venice* — 殘酷但合法的代價
  - 例：The bank wants its pound of flesh.（銀行要討回它的那份代價。）
  - 例：He always demands his pound of flesh.（他總是要把帳算到最後一分。）
  - 補充：莎士比亞《威尼斯商人》中 Shylock 要求借款人還不出錢就割一磅肉。現在比喻「按規矩來但毫不留情的懲罰」
- **dialogue:** The Council needs its pound of flesh.
  - Vander 說議會需要一個人來頂罪——不管 Grayson 個人怎麼想，上面要有人被懲罰，他就是那塊肉。用 "its" 把議會當冰冷的機構，更顯無情

## fall apart
- **phrasal v.** to break into pieces; to collapse or stop functioning — 分崩離析、崩盤
  - 例：The team fell apart after the captain left.（隊長走後整支隊伍就散了。）
  - 例：My life is falling apart.（我的生活正在崩盤。）
  - 例：The old house is falling apart.（那棟老房子快要塌了。）
  - 常見搭配：everything falls apart（一切崩盤）、fall apart at the seams（從接縫處裂開＝徹底崩潰）
- **dialogue:** Without you down here, it all falls apart.
  - Grayson 直說：沒有你在底城壓著，一切都會散掉。底城的秩序不是靠上城法律，是靠 Vander 一個人鎮著

## devilish charm
- **collocation** an attractive quality that has a mischievous, slightly wicked edge — 帶著壞壞味道的魅力、痞帥的吸引力
  - 例：He flashed a smile full of devilish charm.（他露出一個帶著壞壞魅力的笑容。）
  - 補充：devilish = 像魔鬼的，這裡不是真的邪惡，而是那種「壞得讓人喜歡」的味道。常用來形容那種痞帥、不按規矩來但很有個人魅力的人
- **dialogue:** He may not have my devilish charm.
  - Vander 自嘲——Benzo 什麼都好，就是沒有我這種壞帥的魅力。都要被捕了還在開玩笑，這就是他的 devilish charm

## run a tight ship
- **idiom** to manage or control something strictly and efficiently — 管理嚴謹、做事有條理、把事管得滴水不漏
  - 例：The new manager runs a tight ship.（新經理把事情管得很嚴。）
  - 例：She runs a tight ship at home.（她把家裡管得井井有條。）
  - 補充：原意是船長紀律嚴明、船員不敢亂來。用在人身上 = 做事靠譜、不會出亂子
- **dialogue:** He runs a tight ship.
  - Vander 推薦 Benzo 接班：他做事嚴謹、不會讓局面失控。用這個比喻暗示管底城就像管一艘船——需要紀律和控制力

## may not... but...
- **sentence pattern** used to acknowledge a weakness before pivoting to a strength; "雖然沒有⋯但是⋯" — a balanced, fair way to evaluate someone
  - 例：He may not be the smartest, but he works the hardest.（他可能不是最聰明的，但他最努力。）
  - 例：It may not look great, but it works perfectly.（它可能不好看，但用起來完美。）
- **dialogue:** He may not have my devilish charm, but he runs a tight ship.
  - Vander 先自誇一下（Benzo 沒我帥），再認真推薦（但他做事靠譜）。在交代後事的沉重場景裡用幽默包裝，讓氣氛不至於太沉

## won't be + V-ing（委婉語氣）
- **sentence pattern** uses the future continuous to soften a statement; turns a blunt decision/verdict into a gentle description of an upcoming situation — 把直接的宣告變成對未來狀態的柔和描述
  - | 說法 | 語感 |
    |---|---|
    | You **won't come** back. | 很直、很硬——像在宣判 |
    | You **won't be coming** back. | 比較柔——像在陳述一個即將發生的現實 |
  - 例：I won't be needing this anymore.（我不會再需要這個了。）— 比 "I don't need this" 柔和，常見於某人要離開或放棄某物時
  - 例：She won't be joining us tonight.（她今晚不會來了。）— 比 "She won't join us" 客氣，像在傳達一個已定的狀況
  - 例：We won't be seeing each other for a while.（我們會有一段時間見不到面了。）— 比 "We won't see each other" 溫柔，適合對親近的人說
  - 補充：核心差異是 won't be + V-ing 把「決定/判決」變成「描述一個狀態」，語氣不那麼尖銳
- **dialogue:** You won't be coming back for a long time.
  - Grayson 對 Vander 說的。她沒有用冰冷的 "You won't come back"，而是用委婉的方式告訴他一個殘酷的事實——像朋友，不像執法官

## What the devil...
- **informal** an old-fashioned exclamation of shock or surprise; milder variant of "What the hell" — 搞什麼鬼
  - 例：What the devil is going on here?（這裡搞什麼鬼？）
  - 例：What the devil are you doing?（你在搞什麼？）
  - 補充：用 devil（魔鬼）代替 hell（地獄），語氣一樣是震驚但更老派。常見於年紀較長或說話比較文雅的角色
- **dialogue:** What the devil…
  - Benzo 看到 Grayson 被殺的場景時脫口而出——用老派的罵法，符合他這個老江湖的角色

## Go crawl back into whatever hole you came out of.
- **informal** a highly insulting way to tell someone to go back where they came from; implies the person is subhuman and lives in a dirty, hidden place — 滾回你爬出來的洞裡去
  - 例：You're not welcome here. Crawl back into whatever hole you came out of.（你不受歡迎。滾回你來的地方去。）
  - 補充：crawl（爬）暗示對方像蟲蛇、不配用走的；whatever hole（不管什麼洞）表示「我懶得知道也不屑知道」你住哪；整句是全方位的人格否定
- **dialogue:** You animal. Go crawl back into whatever hole you came out of.
  - Benzo 對 Silco 的連環侮辱——你是畜生、你住在洞裡、你是爬行的——每個詞都在否定 Silco 作為人的資格

## never did know
- **sentence pattern** an emphatic form of "never knew"; the added "did" stresses that it has always been true — 就是不知道、從來都不懂（加強語氣）
  - 例：You never did know how to listen.（你就是不會聽人說話。）
  - 例：She never did understand why he left.（她始終不明白他為什麼離開。）
  - 補充：比 "never knew" 多了一層「老毛病、一直以來都是這樣」的語感，像是在評價一個人根深蒂固的缺點
- **dialogue:** You never did know when to walk away.
  - Silco 平靜地評價 Benzo 的老毛病——你從來就不知道什麼時候該撤退。不是嘲笑，是冷靜的事實陳述

## walk away
- **phrasal v.** to leave a situation, especially one that is dangerous or no longer worth pursuing — 抽身、轉身離開、放棄
  - 例：Sometimes you just have to walk away.（有時候你就是得抽身離開。）
  - 例：He walked away from a million-dollar deal.（他放棄了一筆百萬的交易。）
  - 例：She walked away from the argument.（她選擇退出爭吵。）
  - 常見搭配：walk away from（離開⋯、放棄⋯）、walk away unharmed（全身而退）
- **dialogue:** You never did know when to walk away.
  - Silco 說 Benzo 不知道什麼時候該走——不是物理上的「走」，而是「打不過就該撤」的判斷。Benzo 的勇敢就是他的致命傷

## to the end
- **adv.** until the very last moment; all the way through — 直到最後、到底
  - 例：He fought to the end.（他戰鬥到最後一刻。）
  - 例：She was loyal to the end.（她忠誠到底。）
  - 常見搭配：fight to the end（戰到底）、faithful to the end（忠誠到最後）、stubborn to the end（頑固到死）
- **dialogue:** Stubborn to the end.
  - Silco 對 Benzo 的結語——你到死都這麼倔。簡短、冷漠，像在蓋棺定論

## What the hell have you done?
- **informal** an outburst of shock and anger at what someone has done; stronger than "What have you done?" — 你他媽幹了什麼？
  - 例：What the hell have you done to my car?（你對我的車幹了什麼？）
  - 例：What the hell have you done? This can't be fixed!（你搞了什麼？這沒辦法修了！）
  - 補充：what the hell 加在疑問句前面是增強震驚和憤怒，比 what the devil 更粗、更現代
- **dialogue:** What the hell have you done? This wasn't the deal!
  - Marcus 崩潰了——他跟 Silco 有暗盤交易，但沒預料到 Silco 會殺 Grayson。震驚加憤怒加恐懼，一句話暴露了他叛徒的身分

## Deal's changed.
- **informal** short for "The deal has changed"; a cold, unilateral declaration that the agreement no longer applies — 交易變了（我說了算）
  - 例：Sorry, deal's changed. Take it or leave it.（抱歉，條件變了。要就要，不要拉倒。）
  - 補充：用縮寫 "Deal's" 讓整句只有兩個字，冰冷到極點。不是在「協商」變更，是在「通知」你——我已經改了，你沒有選擇
- **dialogue:** Deal's changed.
  - Silco 對 Marcus 暴怒的回應——兩個字，毫不在乎。Marcus 以為自己是合作方，Silco 用這句讓他知道：你只是棋子

## this entire time
- **adv.** for the whole duration up to now; equivalent to "all this time" — 這整段時間以來、一直以來
  - 例：This entire time, I thought you were joking.（我一直以為你在開玩笑。）
  - 例：She was lying to us this entire time.（她這整段時間都在騙我們。）
  - 補充：跟 "all this time" 意思一樣，但 entire 多了一點「從頭到尾、沒有例外」的強調
- **dialogue:** This entire time, I thought I needed to dampen the oscillations.
  - Jayce 恍然大悟——我一直以來的方向都是錯的。this entire time 強調他錯了多久

## on paper
- **adv.** in theory; based on written calculations but not yet proven in practice — 理論上、紙面上
  - 例：The plan looks great on paper.（這個計劃理論上看起來很棒。）
  - 例：On paper, he's the perfect candidate.（紙面上看，他是完美的人選。）
  - 補充：暗示「實際上可能不是這樣」，帶有保留態度
- **dialogue:** It works. — Eh… On paper.
  - Jayce 興奮說「行得通了」，Viktor 冷靜潑冷水——目前只是公式推出來了，還沒實際測試過

## have access to
- **collocation** to be able to use, reach, or enter something — 有權限使用、能接觸到
  - 例：Students have access to the library 24/7.（學生全天候都能使用圖書館。）
  - 例：I don't have access to that file.（我沒有權限開那個檔案。）
  - 常見搭配：have access to information（能取得資訊）、gain access to（取得權限）、deny access to（拒絕進入）
- **dialogue:** We could test it if we had access to my equipment.
  - 注意用了 if we had（假設語氣）——因為設備已經被沒收了，這是「如果能用的話（但不能）」的語氣

## I meant to tell you.
- **sentence pattern** "I originally intended to tell you (but didn't)"; expresses that you planned to do something but failed to follow through — 我本來打算要告訴你的
  - 例：I meant to call you yesterday.（我昨天本來要打給你的。）
  - 例：I meant to tell you sooner, I'm sorry.（我本來想早點告訴你的，抱歉。）
  - 補充：meant to = 原本打算、本來要（但沒做到）。常用於道歉或解釋為什麼漏做了某件事
- **dialogue:** Oh. I… Yeah. I, I meant to tell you.
  - Viktor 結巴了——他早就知道設備要被銷毀卻沒說。meant to 暗示他心裡有愧，但一直沒找到合適的時機

## more than promises
- **collocation** something beyond just words or assurances; implying that actions or evidence are needed — 不只是口頭承諾、光說不夠
  - 例：We need more than promises — we need results.（我們不只要承諾——我們要結果。）
  - 例：The voters want more than promises this time.（選民這次要的不只是空口承諾。）
- **dialogue:** We need more than promises. We need proof.
  - Viktor 務實地打斷 Jayce 的幻想——拿公式去說「你看這行得通」沒用，議會要看到實證

## locked away
- **phrasal v.** to be securely stored or hidden in a place where others cannot access it — 被鎖起來、被收在某處不讓人碰
  - 例：The jewels were locked away in a safe.（珠寶被鎖在保險箱裡。）
  - 例：He kept his feelings locked away.（他把感情深藏心底。）
  - 常見搭配：locked away in（被鎖在⋯裡）、keep something locked away（把某物鎖好）
- **dialogue:** Locked away in Heimerdinger's lab.
  - Viktor 語氣帶暗示——水晶被鎖在漢默丁格的實驗室裡，而他身為院長助理有辦法接近。這句話不是陳述事實，是在提議偷水晶

## Better be right then.
- **sentence pattern** short for "We'd better be right then"; a bold, slightly cocky way to dismiss someone's fear by reframing it as a challenge — 那就最好別錯囉
  - 例：If we only get one shot, better be right then.（如果只有一次機會，那就最好別搞砸。）
  - 補充：省略主詞 we'd，語氣更輕鬆、更挑釁。不是在安慰對方，是在說「怕什麼，做對就好了」
- **dialogue:** Better be right then.
  - Jayce 說「如果我們錯了怎麼辦」，Viktor 一句話把恐懼翻轉成挑戰——那就別錯啊。用最少的字給出最大的膽量

## life's ambition
- **collocation** one's ultimate goal or dream in life; what someone aspires to achieve above all else — 畢生的志向、一生的抱負
  - 例：It was his life's ambition to climb Everest.（攀登聖母峰是他畢生的志向。）
  - 例：She achieved her life's ambition at 30.（她在 30 歲時實現了畢生的志向。）
- **dialogue:** Do you think it was my life's ambition to be an assistant?
  - Viktor 的反問——當助理當然不是我的夢想。他從底城爬上來不是為了幫別人打雜，而是要做真正的科學。Jayce 的研究是他看到的唯一突破口

## I'll admit
- **sentence pattern** a concession phrase used to acknowledge something before moving on; "我承認" — often placed at the end of a sentence
  - 例：It's not my best work, I'll admit.（這不是我最好的作品，我承認。）
  - 例：I'll admit, I was wrong.（我承認，我錯了。）
  - 補充：放在句尾語氣更輕鬆，像是順帶讓步一下。表面上在認錯，但通常接下來會馬上正當化自己的行為
- **dialogue:** It's a little crude, I'll admit.
  - Silco 承認手段粗暴，但語氣毫無歉意——讓步只是為了引出下一句「但這是必要的」

## necessary for
- **collocation** required or essential in order to achieve something — ⋯所必需的
  - 例：Exercise is necessary for good health.（運動是健康所必需的。）
  - 例：Hard work is necessary for success.（努力是成功所必需的。）
  - 常見搭配：necessary for change（變革所需的）、necessary for survival（生存所需的）
- **dialogue:** The base violence necessary for change.
  - Silco 把暴力定位為變革的基本條件——不是「可能有用」，而是「沒有它就不可能改變」

## even with
- **sentence pattern** used to say that something is true despite having a particular advantage — 即使有⋯也⋯、就算有⋯還是⋯
  - 例：Even with all his money, he's not happy.（即使有那麼多錢，他還是不開心。）
  - 例：Even with your help, I can't finish in time.（就算有你幫忙，我也來不及完成。）
- **dialogue:** Even with your monsters, you won't win a war against Piltover.
  - Vander 直接講現實——就算你有怪物也打不贏。實力差距擺在那裡

## set foot in
- **idiom** to enter or visit a place; emphasizes even the smallest act of entering — 踏進、踏入（連一步都⋯）
  - 例：I'll never set foot in that place again.（我再也不會踏進那個地方一步。）
  - 例：She hasn't set foot in the office for weeks.（她已經好幾週沒踏進辦公室了。）
  - 常見搭配：set foot in（踏入）、set foot on（踏上，如 set foot on the moon 踏上月球）
- **dialogue:** They won't dare set foot in the underground again.
  - Silco 的終極目標：讓上城人連底城的邊都不敢碰。set foot in 強調「連一隻腳都不敢放進來」

## get people killed
- **sentence pattern** to indirectly cause people to die through one's actions or decisions; "get + 人 + killed" = 間接導致某人死亡
  - 例：Your recklessness is going to get someone killed.（你的魯莽遲早會害死人。）
  - 例：He got three soldiers killed with that order.（他那個命令害死了三個士兵。）
  - 補充：不是你親手殺的，但因為你的決定或行為導致別人死亡——責任在你身上
- **dialogue:** You'll get people killed. For what?
  - Vander 質問 Silco——你的計劃會害死底城的人。For what? 兩個字的追問，逼 Silco 面對代價

## For what?
- **sentence pattern** a sharp, confrontational way to demand justification; "為了什麼？" — often rhetorical, implying the reason isn't good enough
  - 例：You threw it all away. For what?（你把一切都丟掉了。為了什麼？）
  - 例：We sacrificed everything. For what?（我們犧牲了一切。為了什麼？）
  - 補充：通常不是真的在問，而是在暗示「根本不值得」
- **dialogue:** You'll get people killed. For what? Pride?
  - Vander 質問後自己猜了一個答案（Pride?），但 Silco 糾正：不是面子，是尊重

## shared a vision
- **collocation** to have had a common dream or goal with someone; when used in past tense, implies it no longer exists — 曾有共同的願景
  - 例：We shared a vision for what this company could become.（我們曾經對這間公司的未來有共同的願景。）
  - 例：They once shared a vision, but now they're enemies.（他們曾經有共同的夢想，但現在是敵人。）
- **dialogue:** We shared a vision, Vander. A dream of freedom.
  - shared 用過去式——曾經有共同的夢想，但現在你放棄了，我沒有

## not just... but...
- **sentence pattern** used to expand the scope beyond what was first stated; "不只是⋯而是⋯" — escalates from smaller to larger
  - 例：Not just for me, but for everyone.（不只是為了我，而是為了所有人。）
  - 例：Not just a job, but a calling.（不只是一份工作，而是一種使命。）
- **dialogue:** Not just for the Lanes, but the whole of the underground, united as one.
  - Silco 把格局從巷弄區擴大到整個底城——他要的不是一區的自治，是全底城的統一

## united as one
- **collocation** joined together as a single entity; standing together with shared purpose — 團結為一
  - 例：The people stood united as one against the invaders.（人民團結一致對抗入侵者。）
  - 例：We must be united as one to overcome this.（我們必須團結為一才能克服這個困難。）
- **dialogue:** The whole of the underground, united as one.
  - Silco 的終極目標——整個底城統一成一個國家：Zaun

## Do you even remember?
- **sentence pattern** a confrontational question that implies the other person has forgotten something important; "even" adds doubt and accusation — 你到底還記得嗎？
  - 例：Do you even remember what you promised?（你到底還記不記得你承諾過什麼？）
  - 例：Do you even remember my name?（你到底還記得我的名字嗎？）
  - 補充：even 加了「我懷疑你早就忘了」的語氣，帶著失望和指責
- **dialogue:** Do you even remember?
  - Silco 問 Vander 是否還記得當初的夢想——語氣裡是深深的失望，暗示「你忘了，但我沒忘」

## I've never forgiven myself.
- **sentence pattern** present perfect expressing an ongoing state of guilt from the past to now; "我從來沒有原諒過自己" — the guilt has never stopped
  - 例：I've never forgiven myself for not being there.（我一直沒有原諒自己當時不在場。）
  - 例：She's never forgiven herself for saying those words.（她一直沒有原諒自己說了那些話。）
  - 補充：用現在完成式 have never forgiven 強調「從那時到現在一直都沒有」——不是過去的事，是現在進行中的痛苦
- **dialogue:** I've never forgiven myself. You were my brother.
  - Vander 承認他當年試圖殺 Silco，而且這份愧疚從來沒有消失過。You were my brother 用過去式——你曾是我兄弟，正因如此我更無法原諒自己

## eat through
- **phrasal v.** to corrode or dissolve something by chemical action; to penetrate destructively — 侵蝕穿透、腐蝕掉
  - 例：The acid ate through the metal.（酸液腐蝕穿透了金屬。）
  - 例：Rust ate through the pipe.（鏽蝕穿了管子。）
  - 補充：像酸液一樣慢慢把東西溶掉、穿透——帶有持續破壞的意象
- **dialogue:** The river toxins eat through your nerves.
  - 河裡的毒素侵蝕穿透你的神經——Silco 描述被壓入水中時，毒水腐蝕身體的感覺。這就是他眼睛永久受損的原因

## what you'd done
- **sentence pattern** past perfect (had done) used to refer to an action completed before another past event; "你之前做過的事" — creates a sense of looking back at something even further in the past
  - 例：I couldn't believe what you'd done.（我不敢相信你做了那件事。）
  - 例：She never told anyone what he'd done to her.（她從來沒有告訴任何人他對她做過什麼。）
  - 補充：you'd = you had。用過去完成式表示這件事發生在「恨」之前——先有行為，才有恨意
- **dialogue:** I hated you for what you'd done.
  - Silco 恨 Vander 是因為他「之前做過的事」（把他壓進水裡）。過去完成式把時間線拉清楚：背叛在先，恨在後

## as time passed, so did...
- **sentence pattern** a parallel structure meaning "as X happened, Y happened too"; "so did" is an inverted form expressing that something else also passed/changed — 隨著時間過去，⋯也跟著過去了
  - 例：As his confidence grew, so did his ambition.（隨著他的自信成長，他的野心也跟著增長。）
  - 例：As the days passed, so did the pain.（隨著日子過去，痛苦也跟著消退了。）
  - 補充：so did 是倒裝結構，表示「⋯也一樣」。整句的對稱感帶有文學性
- **dialogue:** But as time passed, so did my hate.
  - 時間流逝了，恨意也同樣消逝了。用對稱的結構表達兩件事同步消失——非常有詩意的說法

## was left with
- **sentence pattern** to be the only thing remaining after everything else is gone; "最後剩下的是⋯" — implies a process of loss or change that leaves something behind
  - 例：After the fire, we were left with nothing.（火災之後，我們什麼都不剩。）
  - 例：She was left with a deep sense of loss.（她最後剩下的只有深深的失落感。）
- **dialogue:** And I was left with an understanding.
  - 恨消失之後，留下來的不是空虛，是一個領悟。understanding 在這裡不是「理解」，而是用差點被殺的代價換來的清醒認知

## stop at nothing
- **idiom** to be willing to do anything to achieve a goal, no matter how extreme or unethical — 不擇手段、什麼都願意做
  - 例：She'll stop at nothing to get that promotion.（她為了升遷什麼都願意做。）
  - 例：He stopped at nothing to protect his family.（他為了保護家人不擇手段。）
  - 補充：字面意思是「在任何事面前都不會停下」——沒有底線、沒有不敢做的事
- **dialogue:** The only way to defeat a superior enemy is to stop at nothing.
  - Silco 從溺水中悟出的核心哲學——面對比你強的對手，唯一的出路就是沒有底線

## kept my respect
- **collocation** to have maintained someone's respect over time; "kept" implies it was ongoing until something changed — 一直保有我的尊重
  - 例：Despite everything, he kept my respect.（儘管一切，他仍保有我的尊重。）
  - 補充：kept 是過去式，暗示「曾經保有但後來失去了」——後面通常會接一個 until 說明轉折點
- **dialogue:** I hated you, but you kept my respect. Until you made peace with them.
  - Silco 即使恨 Vander 也敬重他——因為當年的 Vander 是戰士。但跟上城講和的那一刻，尊重就沒了

## made peace with
- **collocation** to end a conflict and reach a peaceful agreement with someone; to reconcile — 跟⋯講和、跟⋯和解
  - 例：He finally made peace with his past.（他終於跟自己的過去和解了。）
  - 例：The two countries made peace after years of war.（兩國在多年戰爭後講和了。）
- **dialogue:** Until you made peace with them.
  - Vander 跟上城講和——在 Silco 眼中這是最大的背叛。他們一起打過仗、流過血，結果你去跟敵人握手？

## played lapdog
- **collocation** to act as someone's obedient servant; to submit to authority without resistance — 當走狗、卑躬屈膝
  - 例：He played lapdog to the CEO for years.（他當了老闆的走狗好幾年。）
  - 補充：played 帶有「扮演」的語感——你在演一個走狗的角色，這不是真正的你。既是侮辱又是在說「你背叛了自己」
- **dialogue:** Played lapdog after everything we suffered.
  - Silco 最重的指控——在我們承受了那麼多痛苦之後，你居然去當壓迫者的走狗

## realize our dream
- **collocation** to make a dream become reality; to achieve what was once only imagined — 實現我們的夢想
  - 例：She finally realized her dream of becoming a pilot.（她終於實現了成為飛行員的夢想。）
  - 例：It took years, but we realized our dream.（花了好幾年，但我們實現了夢想。）
  - 補充：realize 在這裡是「使成真」，不是「意識到」
- **dialogue:** We can finally realize our dream, brother.
  - Silco 的最後拉攏——有了微光，我們終於可以實現當年的夢想了。叫 Vander「兄弟」是最後的溫情牌

## everything that we are
- **sentence pattern** refers not to possessions but to identity, values, and essence; "我們的本質、我們之所以為我們的一切"
  - 例：If we lose our integrity, we lose everything that we are.（如果失去正直，我們就失去了一切。）
  - 例：This place is everything that we are.（這個地方就是我們的全部。）
  - 補充：that we are 限定的不是「擁有的東西」，而是「我們這個人的本質」
- **dialogue:** In fighting topside, you'd sacrifice everything that we are.
  - Vander 最後的立場——你的戰爭會毀掉的不只是生命，而是底城人的靈魂。贏了戰爭但失去本質，那還剩什麼？

## So far, so good.
- **idiom** everything has been fine up to this point; often implies uncertainty about what comes next — 目前為止一切順利（但接下來就不好說了）
  - 例：How's the new job? — So far, so good.（新工作怎樣？——目前為止還不錯。）
  - 例：The surgery went well. So far, so good.（手術順利。目前為止一切正常。）
  - 補充：常帶有「但還不能放心」的潛台詞
- **dialogue:** So far, so good.
  - Viktor 溜進實驗室還沒被發現，先鬆口氣——結果下一秒 Mel 就出現了

## quite the + 名詞
- **sentence pattern** an emphatic way to express that something is impressive, notable, or remarkable; "可不小的⋯、相當厲害的⋯"
  - 例：That's quite the accomplishment.（那可是不小的成就。）
  - 例：She's quite the character.（她可真是個人物。）
  - 例：That was quite the performance.（那場表演可真精彩。）
  - 補充：帶有讚嘆或調侃的語氣，比 "very" 更有味道
- **dialogue:** That's quite the conviction.
  - Mel 評價 Jayce 的膽量——被判了罪還敢來偷東西，這份信念「可不小」

## What a surprise to see you
- **sentence pattern** used to express (real or fake) surprise at encountering someone; often sarcastic — 看到你真是太意外了
  - 例：What a surprise to see you here!（在這裡看到你真意外！）
  - 例：Oh, what a surprise to see you at a party.（噢，在派對上看到你真意外。）
  - 補充：語氣決定一切——真心驚喜或完全反話都可以
- **dialogue:** What a surprise to see you, huh?
  - Jayce 被 Mel 抓到偷東西，瞬間切換成裝熱情模式——「哎呀你也在！」拙劣的社交掩飾

## How is ... any different?
- **sentence pattern** a challenging question that implies there is no difference; "any" strengthens the doubt — ⋯又有什麼不同？（暗示沒有不同）
  - 例：How is this plan any different from the last one?（這個計劃跟上一個有什麼不同？）
  - 例：How is today any different from yesterday?（今天跟昨天有什麼不一樣？）
  - 補充：any 在疑問句中加強語氣——有「任何」一點不同嗎？通常暗示答案是「沒有」
- **dialogue:** You couldn't do so earlier today, how is tonight any different?
  - Mel 的邏輯很犀利——你白天在議會上都說服不了人，現在半夜來偷東西就能成功了？給我一個理由

## do so
- **sentence pattern** a formal substitute for repeating a previously mentioned verb phrase; avoids repetition — 這麼做、做到那件事（代替前面提過的動詞片語）
  - 例：You may leave if you wish to do so.（如果你想走的話可以走。）
  - 例：I asked him to help, but he refused to do so.（我請他幫忙，但他拒絕了。）
  - 補充：so 代替前面整個動作（這裡是 prove that it works），避免重複，讓句子更簡潔
- **dialogue:** You couldn't do so earlier today.
  - do so = prove that it works。Mel 不重複 Jayce 的話，直接用 do so 帶過——語氣更俐落
